## Brachytherapy Recommendations

### Low Risk
- Active surveillance (preferred) OR
- EBRT (moderate hypofractionation or conventional) OR
- LDR brachytherapy (standard option)
- HDR brachytherapy monotherapy (alternative option, especially in larger glands)
- Radical prostatectomy (option)

### Favorable Intermediate Risk
- Active surveillance (some)
- Radical prostatectomy with PLND
- EBRT + ADT (4-6 months)
- LDR brachytherapy alone
- HDR brachytherapy monotherapy
- EBRT + LDR or HDR boost (alternative)

### Unfavorable Intermediate Risk
- EBRT (70-78 Gy) + ADT (4-6 months) preferred
- EBRT + LDR or HDR boost is an option
- RP + extended PLND

### High Risk
- EBRT (70-78 Gy to prostate + SV) + ADT (12-36 months) + brachytherapy boost (LDR or HDR)
- Or EBRT + ADT without boost

## Key Findings
- Brachytherapy is a standard of care for low and favorable intermediate risk
- LDR monotherapy for low risk has 10-year bNED >90%
- EBRT + brachytherapy boost improves biochemical control for intermediate/high risk
- Decision-making should be multi-disciplinary
- Patient preference and quality-of-life tradeoffs important
