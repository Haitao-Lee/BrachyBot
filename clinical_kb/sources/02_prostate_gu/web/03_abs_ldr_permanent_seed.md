## Patient Selection
### Ideal Candidates (LDR Monotherapy)
- Low risk (PSA <10, GS ≤6, ≤cT2a)
- Favorable intermediate risk (single intermediate-risk factor, GS 3+4, <50% cores)
- Prostate volume <50 cc (TRUS)
- IPSS <15-20
- No prior TURP (relative)
- Life expectancy >10 years

### Combined EBRT + LDR Boost
- Intermediate risk (unfavorable)
- High risk (selected)
- 110 Gy I-125 boost after 37.5-50 Gy EBRT

## Prescriptions (TG-43 / NIST-99)

### I-125 (model 6711, OncoSeed)
- Monotherapy: 144 Gy (TG-43)
- Boost: 108-110 Gy

### Pd-103 (model 200)
- Monotherapy: 125 Gy
- Boost: 90-100 Gy

### Cs-131 (model CS-1)
- Monotherapy: 115 Gy
- Boost: 85 Gy

## Intraoperative Planning
- TRUS-guided (5 mm step)
- Real-time inverse planning (modified peripheral loading)
- 70-90 seeds typical for monotherapy
- 25-35 needles for LDR
- Loose seeds or stranded (linked) seeds
- Stranded seeds preferred (less migration)

## Post-Implant Dosimetry (Day 30 CT)
- D90 (minimum dose to 90% prostate): 100% (140-160 Gy typical I-125 mono)
- V100: >95% (target 98%)
- V150: 30-50%
- V200: <20%
- Rectum V100 <1 cc
- Urethra D10 <150% (≤217 Gy I-125)
- Urethra D30 <130%

## Quality Indicators
- Day 30 CT for dosimetric analysis
- CT-MRI fusion optional for soft-tissue definition
- CTV = prostate (no margin)
- PTV = CTV (no margin for LDR)

## Outcomes
- 10-year bNED: 85-95% (low risk), 75-85% (intermediate)
- Grade 3+ late GU: 5-10%
- Grade 3+ late GI: <2%
- Long-term erectile preservation: 60-80%

## Key Findings
- LDR monotherapy is standard for low-risk prostate cancer
- Real-time TRUS planning preferred
- Day 30 CT dosimetry is mandatory
- Stranded seeds reduce embolic events
- Higher D90 (>140 Gy) improves control; >180 Gy increases toxicity
