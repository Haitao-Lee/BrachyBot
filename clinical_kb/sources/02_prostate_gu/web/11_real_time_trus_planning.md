## Concept
- Inverse planning during implant (vs. pre-plan or post-plan only)
- Modified peripheral loading (MPZL) historically; now inverse planning
- Continuous needle-by-needle optimization

## Steps

### 1. Pre-Implant
- mpMRI + CT for prostate volume, OAR anatomy
- Lithotomy position
- TRUS probe (BK, Hitachi)
- 5 mm stepper with template
- Axial + sagittal (sometimes biplane)

### 2. Contouring
- Prostate, urethra (TRUS-visible), rectum
- Sometimes bladder neck
- MRI-TRUS fusion if available

### 3. Needles
- 17-19 G needles, 20 cm
- 13-22 needles (depends on volume)
- Parallel placement via template

### 4. Inverse Planning
- IPSA / HIPO algorithm
- Real-time dose calculation
- Update as each needle placed

### 5. Seed Loading
- LDR: Mick applicator (loose) or pre-loaded strands
- HDR: transfer cables to afterloader

## Quality Goals
- D90: 100% (140-160 Gy I-125)
- V100: >95%
- V150: 30-50%
- Urethra D10 < 150% Rx
- Rectum V100 < 1 cc

## Advantages of Real-Time
- Adjusts for intra-op prostate motion/edema
- Higher D90 achievable
- Lower seed migration
- Comparable to or better than pre-planning

## Key Findings
- Real-time TRUS planning is the modern standard
- Inverse planning (IPSA) outperforms manual MPZL
- Day 30 CT remains the dosimetric reference
- Average 70-90 seeds for monotherapy, 25-35 for boost
- Strand-loaded seeds reduce embolic events
