## Epidemiology / Background
- Penile cancer is rare (<1% of male cancers in Western countries)
- Higher incidence in Brazil, India, parts of Africa
- Risk factors: HPV, phimosis, smoking, lack of circumcision

## Indication for Brachytherapy
- T1-T2 penile carcinoma (≤4 cm lesions, no urethral involvement)
- Preservation of organ function (vs. partial/total penectomy)
- Interstitial BT preferred for lesions >1 cm depth
- Surface mold for very superficial (≤5 mm depth)

## Technique (Interstitial)
- Ir-192 HDR (preferred) or LDR
- TRUS or ultrasound-guided needle insertion
- Parallel needle array (1-1.5 cm spacing)
- Typical 4-8 needles for lesions 1-3 cm
- Plastic tubes for LDR

## Dose/Fractionation

### HDR
- 38-44 Gy in 10-12 fractions, 2×/day (5-6 days)
- 3.0-3.4 Gy per fraction, BID, 6-hour interval

### LDR (Ir-192 wire or seeds)
- 60-70 Gy in 4-7 days (continuous, 0.4-0.5 Gy/h)

### PDR
- 60-70 Gy total, 0.6-0.8 Gy pulse, hourly

## Target Volume
- GTV = visible/palpable tumor
- CTV = GTV + 5-10 mm margin
- CTV may be limited at corpus cavernosum boundary
- For surface: CTV = clinical lesion + 5 mm radial margin

## Dose Constraints
- Urethra: max dose ≤120% prescription
- Skin surface: ≤80% prescription
- Contralateral uninvolved tissue: minimize

## Outcomes (5-year)
- Local control: 80-90% (T1), 65-75% (T2)
- Penile preservation: 80-85%
- Sexual function: variable preservation
- Meatal stenosis: 5-15%
- Necrosis: 5-10%
- Salvage surgery effective for failures

## Key Findings
- Interstitial HDR brachytherapy is the standard for organ preservation
- 60-70 Gy in 7-10 days (HDR) is typical
- 5-year local control 80% for T1-2 lesions
- Brachytherapy is the most effective non-surgical option for penile preservation
- Multi-disciplinary follow-up essential
