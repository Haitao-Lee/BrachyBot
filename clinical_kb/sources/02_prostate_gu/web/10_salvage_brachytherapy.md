## Background
- 30-40% of EBRT patients fail biochemically within 10 years
- Local salvage options: cryotherapy, HIFU, salvage prostatectomy, salvage BT
- Brachytherapy allows very high focal dose to recurrent lesion

## Patient Selection
- Confirmed local recurrence (mpMRI, PSMA-PET, biopsy)
- Long PSA-DT (>9-12 months)
- Initial low-to-intermediate risk
- >2-3 years since prior EBRT
- No metastases
- No rectal fistula history

## Technique Options

### LDR Salvage (Pd-103 preferred — short half-life)
- 90-110 Gy Pd-103
- Lower dose due to previous EBRT
- Post-implant CT day 30

### HDR Salvage
- Single implant
- 19-24 Gy / 1-2 fractions (after prior full EBRT 70-78 Gy)
- TRUS-guided

## Dose (Current Consensus)
- HDR: 19 Gy × 1, 12-13.5 Gy × 2, or 8-10 Gy × 3
- LDR: 90-100 Gy Pd-103

## Dose Constraints (HDR Salvage, Single 19 Gy)
- Rectum D2cc < 9-10 Gy
- Bladder D2cc < 12 Gy
- Urethra D10 < 19 Gy (≤100% prescription)

## Outcomes (Selected Series)
- 5-year bNED: 50-70% (HDR salvage)
- 5-year local control: 70-85%
- 5-year MFS: 60-80%
- Late Grade 3+ GU: 15-25%
- Late Grade 3+ GI: 5-10%
- Rectal fistula: 1-3% (high-risk complication)
- Urinary retention: 10-20%

## Toxicity Concerns
- Cumulative dose to rectum: total EBRT + salvage BT
- Risk of fistula (most serious complication)
- Urinary incontinence 10-15%
- Higher toxicity than primary BT

## Key Findings
- Salvage BT is a curative option for carefully selected patients
- 5-year bNED ~60% in good-candidate patients
- Rectal fistula is the most feared complication
- HDR salvage is increasingly preferred over LDR (better OAR sparing)
- Multi-disciplinary evaluation mandatory
