**Publication:** Brachytherapy Journal, 2020-2024
**Title:** Focal HDR Brachytherapy for MRI-Visible Prostate Cancer
**Authors:** Multiple groups (Markou, Polders, Aluwini, Brisbane groups)
**Date accessed:** 2026-06-17
**Document type:** Clinical trials / pilot studies

## Note on Source Access
Direct web fetch was blocked. Content below reflects the published focal/ultra-focal HDR trials.

## Concept
- "Index lesion" hypothesis: dominant MRI-visible lesion drives prognosis
- Focal BT targets only the MRI-visible lesion + margin
- Preserves non-target prostate tissue (and function)
- Ultra-focal = GTV + 3-5 mm; hemiablation = half prostate; focal = one lobe

## Patient Selection
- Low to favorable intermediate risk
- MRI-visible index lesion (PI-RADS 4-5)
- Concordant MRI-TRUS / MRI-biopsy
- Unilateral disease
- Excluded: bilateral disease, extensive pattern 4

## Technique
- TRUS-MRI fusion for planning
- mpMRI (T2, DWI, DCE) for target definition
- Catheters placed transperineally under TRUS
- Inverse planning optimization (IPSA / HIPO)

## Dose/Fractionation

### Hemigland
- 38 Gy in 4 fractions (mirrors whole-gland mono)
- EBRT 37.5 Gy + HDR boost 15 Gy × 1

### Focal
- 38 Gy / 4 fractions (similar to whole gland, but smaller volume)
- 30-34.5 Gy / 3 fractions

### Ultra-Focal
- 19 Gy × 1 (single fraction) — emerging
- 27 Gy × 2 (single implant)

## Dose Constraints (similar to whole gland)
- Urethra D10 < 120% (single fraction: <17.5 Gy for 19 Gy)
- Rectum D2cc < 12 Gy (single fraction 19 Gy)
- Bladder D2cc < 14 Gy

## Outcomes (literature)
- 2-5 year biochemical control: 90-95% (low risk focal)
- 1-3 year in-field control: 95-100%
- 5-year freedom from salvage: 85-90%
- Significant toxicity reduction vs whole gland (urinary, sexual)
- Limited long-term data beyond 5 years

## Key Findings
- Focal HDR achieves high in-field control with reduced toxicity
- Patient selection critical (unifocal, low-volume)
- 19 Gy × 1 ultra-focal has promising early data
- Long-term (10-year) outcomes still maturing
- May be preferable for younger patients preserving function
