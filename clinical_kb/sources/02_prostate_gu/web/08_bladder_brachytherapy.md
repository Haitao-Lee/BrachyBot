## Background
- Muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC): standard = radical cystectomy
- Bladder-preserving trimodality therapy (TURBT + chemo-RT) alternative
- Brachytherapy as boost or sole RT for selected patients
- Used in European centers (Netherlands, France) — limited US adoption

## Indications
- T1-T2 (some T3a) urothelial carcinoma
- Solitary lesion <5 cm
- No multifocal disease
- No hydronephrosis
- Visible complete TURBT
- Solitary kidney or medical inoperability

## Brachytherapy Techniques

### Multicatheter Interstitial (perineal template)
- Open cystotomy with direct visualization
- Afterloading catheters placed through bladder wall
- Under TRUS or CT guidance
- Suitable for posterior/lateral wall lesions
- 5-7 day implant

### Intraoperative LDR (Ta or T1)
- Direct intravesical LDR seed placement
- Limited applicability
- HDR intravesical applicator (some)

## Dose/Fractionation

### Multicatheter HDR Boost (after EBRT)
- EBRT 40-45 Gy to bladder + nodes
- HDR boost 20-30 Gy in 4-6 fractions over 2-3 days
- Total EQD2 ~70-80 Gy

### LDR
- 40-60 Gy continuous LDR over 4-7 days (with EBRT)

## Dose Constraints
- Rectum (posterior): D2cc < 70 Gy
- Bowel small: D2cc < 60 Gy
- Femoral heads: D2cc < 50 Gy
- Bladder mucosa outside CTV: minimize

## Outcomes
- 5-year local control: 60-80% (T2), 50-65% (T3)
- 5-year bladder preservation: 75-85%
- 5-year OS: 50-65% (selected T2)
- Late toxicity: 10-20% (hematuria, contracted bladder)

## Key Findings
- Bladder BT is feasible for selected MIBC
- Best for solitary lesions <5 cm
- Higher dose than EBRT achievable
- Requires surgical expertise for catheter placement
- Limited US adoption, more common in Europe
