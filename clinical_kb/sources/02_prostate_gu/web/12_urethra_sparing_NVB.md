## Urethra Dosing (LDR and HDR)

### Anatomy
- Prostatic urethra visualized on TRUS
- Membranous urethra (at apex) — high-dose risk for incontinence
- Bladder neck — high-dose risk for retention

### Standard Dose Limits (LDR I-125)
- D10 (urethra) < 150% prescription (≤217 Gy I-125 mono)
- D30 < 130%
- V150 (urethra) < 5-10%
- Urethral V100 < 95%

### Standard Dose Limits (HDR)
- D10 < 120% prescription
- D0.1cc < 130%
- D30 < 105% (monotherapy)

## Clinical Impact
- D10 > 150% → increased urethral stricture (5-10% >5 years)
- High urethral dose → urinary frequency, urgency, dysuria
- Risk of incontinence (especially with prior TURP)

## Neurovascular Bundles (NVB)
- Posterolateral to prostate (5 and 7 o'clock)
- Carries cavernous nerves
- Associated with erectile function
- Imaging: MRI T2-weighted

### Dose Constraints (proposed)
- D50 (NVB) < 100-150% (LDR) — no universal consensus
- Dmean < 50-100 Gy EQD2
- Erectile function preservation 60-80% at 5 years (vs 30-50% with high dose)

## Penile Bulb
- Not a strong predictor of erectile function (poor surrogate)
- D70 < 70 Gy EQD2 (general limit)

## Optimization Strategies
- Inverse planning with urethral constraint
- Needle avoidance in peri-urethral zone
- MRI-based urethra delineation
- Loaded needles avoiding urethra line

## Key Findings
- D10 urethra is the primary urethral constraint
- MRI-planned HDR allows better urethral sparing than TRUS
- NVB dose may impact erectile function but consensus is weak
- Trade-off between target coverage and OAR sparing
- 90% of failures are NOT at urethra, so sparing the urethra is generally safe
