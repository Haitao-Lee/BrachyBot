## Study Design
- 398 patients with intermediate- and high-risk prostate cancer
- Randomized 1:1
- Arm A (DE-EBRT): 12 mo ADT + EBRT 78 Gy in 39 fractions (pelvic nodal)
- Arm B (LDR boost): 12 mo ADT + EBRT 46 Gy in 23 fractions + LDR I-125 boost (110 Gy prescription)
- Primary endpoint: biochemical failure (Phoenix: nadir + 2 ng/mL)
- Secondary: OS, CSS, MFS, late toxicity

## Key 6-Year Results

### Biochemical Control
- 7-year bNED: LDR boost 86% vs EBRT 75% (p<0.001)
- Median follow-up 6.5 years
- Hazard ratio ~0.49 (significant)
- 5-year bNED difference: ~15% absolute

### Survival
- No significant OS difference at 6 years
- No significant CSS difference at 6 years
- Trend toward improved MFS with LDR boost

### Late Toxicity (Grade ≥3)
- GU toxicity higher with LDR boost (cumulative incidence ~20% vs 8%)
- GI toxicity similar between arms
- LDR boost associated with higher urinary retention rates

### Dose
- LDR boost 110 Gy I-125 to whole prostate
- Combined EQD2 (α/β=1.5): ~125 Gy
- EBRT 78 Gy alone EQD2 (α/β=1.5): ~115 Gy

## Key Findings
- LDR boost significantly improves biochemical control in intermediate/high-risk
- Trade-off: improved oncologic outcome with increased late urinary toxicity
- LDR boost remains a standard option for dose escalation
- Supports LDR as an alternative to HDR boost for dose escalation
- I-125 prescription: 110 Gy as boost, 144 Gy as monotherapy
