## Indications for HDR
- Monotherapy: low + favorable intermediate risk
- Boost: intermediate + high risk with EBRT (37.5-46 Gy)

## Dose/Fractionation (GEC-ESTRO)

### HDR Monotherapy
- 38 Gy in 4 fractions (most common, 2 implants)
- 34.5 Gy in 3 fractions
- 27 Gy in 2 fractions (single implant, 6 hours apart)
- 19 Gy in 1 fraction (emerging, ultra-hypofractionated)

### HDR Boost (combined with EBRT)
- 15 Gy × 1 (single fraction)
- 10-15 Gy × 2 (two fractions)
- 9-10 Gy × 3 (rare)
- 5.5-7.25 Gy × 4 (rare)

## Dose Constraints (EQD2 with α/β=3 for OARs)

### Rectum
- D2cc < 74 Gy (EQD2) — single-fraction: D2cc < 12 Gy
- D0.1cc < 78 Gy

### Bladder
- D2cc < 80 Gy (EQD2)
- D0.1cc < 100 Gy

### Urethra
- D10 < 120% prescription (single-fraction: D10 < 17.5 Gy for 19 Gy)
- D0.1cc < 130%
- D30 < 105%

### Penile Bulb
- D70 < 70 Gy (no strict constraint)

## Implant Procedure
- TRUS-guided transperineal
- 13-19 catheters (typical 17)
- Single-plane or biplane TRUS
- CT or MRI for planning
- Inverse planning with HIPO (Hybrid Inverse Planning Optimization)

## Imaging
- Pre-implant MRI (multiparametric) for target
- Post-implant CT or MRI for dosimetry
- Day 0 or Day 1 dosimetry for HDR

## Key Findings
- 38 Gy / 4 fractions is a common European monotherapy regimen
- HIPO inverse planning is widely adopted
- Single-fraction 19 Gy HDR monotherapy has 5-year data showing favorable control
- MRI-based planning improves urethral sparing
- EBRT + HDR boost achieves higher EQD2 than EBRT alone
