## Key Recommendations

### Patient Selection (HDR Boost)
- Intermediate-risk and selected high-risk prostate cancer
- Combined with external beam radiotherapy (EBRT) 37.5-50 Gy
- HDR boost delivered before, during, or after EBRT
- Adequate rectal/prostate spacing or use of rectal spacer

### Dose/Fractionation (ABS 2022)
- Single-fraction HDR boost: 15 Gy (one implant, one fraction) — investigational
- Two-fraction HDR boost: 13.5-15 Gy × 2 fractions (6-8 hours apart)
- EBRT typically 37.5-50 Gy in 15-25 fractions
- Total EQD2 (α/β=1.5): 110-120 Gy

### Dose/Fractionation (HDR Monotherapy)
- 38 Gy in 4 fractions (2 implants, 1 week apart)
- 34.5 Gy in 3 fractions
- 27 Gy in 2 fractions (single implant, 2 fractions)
- 19 Gy in 1 fraction (single implant, single fraction) — emerging

### Dose Constraints (D90 / V100)
- Prostate V100 > 95%
- Prostate D90 > 100% prescription (target)
- Prostate V150 < 35-50%
- Prostate V200 < 20%

### Organ-at-Risk Dose Limits
- Urethra D10 < 120% (typically < 130%)
- Urethra V150 < 5-10%
- Rectum V75 < 1 cc, D2cc < 75 Gy EQD2
- Bladder D2cc < 80 Gy EQD2
- Penile bulb: not strictly constrained; minimize dose

### Technical Recommendations
- Real-time transrectal ultrasound (TRUS) guidance
- CT-based or MR-based planning (MR preferred for urethra visualization)
- Inverse planning with dose constraints optimization
- 13-17 catheters typical

### Post-Implant Assessment
- CT at day 30 for LDR
- CT or MRI immediately post-implant for HDR
- MRI preferred for soft-tissue delineation

## Key Findings
- HDR boost is standard of care option for intermediate-risk
- Brachytherapy provides lowest α/β correction, optimal for prostate
- Monotherapy reserved for low-risk and favorable intermediate-risk
- Focal HDR emerging for MRI-visible dominant lesion
