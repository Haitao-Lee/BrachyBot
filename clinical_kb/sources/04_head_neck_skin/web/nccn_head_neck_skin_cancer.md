## NCCN H&N Cancers (Brachytherapy Role)
- **General statement**: "Brachytherapy alone or as a boost is an appropriate treatment option for selected patients with early-stage (T1–T2) head and neck cancers, particularly of the oral cavity and lip"
- **Recommended indications**:
  - T1–T2 N0 oral cavity (tongue, floor of mouth, buccal, lip)
  - Boost to EBRT for T3–T4 disease in selected cases
  - Salvage of recurrent disease
  - Re-irradiation (with caution)
- **Technique options** (per NCCN): interstitial (catheter-based), intracavitary, surface mold
- **Dose**: institutional preference, generally 60–70 Gy LDR equivalent, 50–55 Gy HDR in 15–20 fx as monotherapy, or 15–25 Gy boost

## NCCN Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)
- Brachytherapy is listed as a Category 2A option for:
  - Primary BCC where surgery is contraindicated or refused
  - Adjuvant treatment of high-risk BCC (positive margins, perineural invasion, recurrent)
  - Special sites (face H, "mask area"): nose, eyelids, ears, lips
- Surface applicators (Leipzig, Freiburg, custom 3D-printed) recommended for lesions < 5 mm depth
- Interstitial for thicker lesions

## NCCN Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) of Skin
- Brachytherapy is Category 2A for:
  - Locally advanced SCC where surgery would be disfiguring
  - Recurrent SCC
  - Post-operative adjuvant (high-risk features)
  - Perineural invasion
- Dose: 50–55 Gy in 15–20 fx (HDR surface)
- Or 64 Gy in 32 fx BID (electron-like schedule)

## Comparative Effectiveness Notes
- Surgery remains standard of care for most NMSC
- BT offers comparable LC to EBRT for superficial lesions
- Advantages of BT: shorter treatment course, sparing of deeper structures, better cosmesis in selected sites
- Disadvantages: technical complexity, need for specialized center

## Dose Recommendations Summary
| Site | Modality | Dose |
|---|---|---|
| Oral cavity T1–T2 | Interstitial | 50–55 Gy / 15–20 fx HDR or 60–65 Gy LDR |
| Oral cavity T3 (boost) | Interstitial | 18–24 Gy / 3–6 fx HDR after 50 Gy EBRT |
| Lip T1–T2 | Interstitial / surface | 50–60 Gy / 10–15 fx HDR |
| Skin BCC/SCC < 5 mm | Surface | 50–55 Gy / 15–20 fx HDR |
| Skin BCC/SCC > 5 mm | Interstitial | 50–55 Gy / 15–20 fx HDR |
| NPC persistent (boost) | Intracavitary | 5–8 Gy × 2–3 fx |
| Recurrent H&N | Interstitial | 30–40 Gy / 8–10 fx HDR |
