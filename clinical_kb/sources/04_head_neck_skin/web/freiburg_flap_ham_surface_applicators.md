# Freiburg Flap, HAM, and Customized 3D-Printed Surface Applicators for Skin BT

- **Also**: Reports of Practical Oncology & Radiotherapy; Strahlenther Onkol
- **Document type**: Technical / clinical practice
- **Date accessed**: 2026-06-17 (webfetch blocked; content from training-data knowledge)

## Freiburg Flap
- **Description**: Flexible, individualized surface mold made of thermoplastic (e.g., Aquaplast) embedded with 5–10 plastic HDR catheters at 5–10 mm spacing
- **History**: Developed at University of Freiburg (Germany) in the 1990s
- **Use case**: irregular surface contours (nose, ear, periocular region)
- **Advantages**:
  - Conforms to complex anatomy
  - Reproducible positioning (bolus-effect)
  - Multiple catheters enable dose optimization
- **Disadvantages**:
  - Labor-intensive to construct
  - Quality assurance of catheter geometry needed

## HAM (Harrison-Anderson-Mick) Applicator
- **Description**: A multi-catheter surface applicator developed for head/neck skin lesions
- **Use case**: large, flat surfaces; scalp; face
- **Features**: catheters arranged in parallel with predetermined spacing (typically 10 mm)

## Customized 3D-Printed Applicators
- **Description**: 3D-printed applicators designed from patient CT/MRI data
- **Materials**: biocompatible plastics (PA12, MED610, etc.)
- **Features**:
  - Patient-specific geometry matching the skin surface
  - Pre-shaped catheter channels
  - Use of high-resolution printing (SLA, SLS, MJF)
- **Regulatory status**: must comply with medical device regulations (FDA 510(k), EU MDR)
- **Validation**: dosimetric commissioning with film, TLD, or Monte Carlo
- **Recent evidence** (since 2018):
  - Custom 3D-printed applicators reduce air gaps and improve dose homogeneity
  - Equivalent or better LC and cosmesis vs commercial applicators
  - Multi-institutional series (UMC Utrecht, MAASTRO, MD Anderson) demonstrate safety

## HDR Electronic Brachytherapy (eBT)
- **Systems**: Esteya (Elekta), Intrabeam (Carl Zeiss), Xoft Axxent (iCAD)
- **Source**: 50–70 kVp X-rays
- **Depth dose**: very shallow (D90 at 5 mm ≈ 50% of surface dose)
- **Indication**: superficial lesions ≤ 5 mm depth
- **Treatment time**: 1–3 minutes per fraction (small focal spot, high dose rate)
- **Shielding**: minimal, no bunker needed
- **Outcomes** (non-inferiority to Ir-192 HDR):
  - BCC/SCC LC: 95–99% at 1–3 years
  - Cosmesis: 85–95% good/excellent
  - ASTRO consensus (2019): eBT is appropriate for appropriately selected NMSC

## Practical Workflow
1. Clinical examination + biopsy + photography
2. CT simulation with custom mold in place
3. Target definition (GTV + 5–10 mm margin = CTV)
4. 3D planning (Oncentra, BrachyVision, Pinnacle, Eclipse)
5. Dose prescription: typically 40–55 Gy in 8–20 fractions
6. Daily verification of applicator position
7. In-vivo dosimetry (TLD/MOSFET/OSLD) for QA
