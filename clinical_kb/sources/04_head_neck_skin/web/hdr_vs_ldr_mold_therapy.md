## Physics Comparison
| Parameter | HDR (Ir-192) | LDR (Ir-192 wire) | LDR (Cs-137) | PDR (Ir-192) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Source | 192Ir, 10 Ci | 192Ir wire, 1–10 Ci/cm | 137Cs tube | 192Ir, 1 Ci |
| Dose rate | > 12 Gy/h | 0.4–1.0 Gy/h | 0.4–1.0 Gy/h | 0.5–1.0 Gy/h (pulses) |
| Energy | 380 keV avg | 380 keV avg | 660 keV | 380 keV avg |
| Half-life | 73.8 d | 73.8 d | 30 yr | 73.8 d |
| Step size | 5 mm (typical) | n/a (continuous) | n/a | 5 mm |
| Treatment time | 5–30 min/fx | 4–7 d | 4–7 d | 1 h pulses, hourly |
| Radiobiology (LQ) | α/β-dependent | Continuously low | Continuous | Hybrid |
| Optimization | Inverse (IPSA) | Manual Paris | Manual | Inverse + pulses |

## LDR Advantages
- Continuous low dose rate (radiobiologically favorable for late-responding tissue)
- Less optimization sensitivity (more forgiving to source position error)
- Decades of clinical experience (Paris system)
- 4–7 days inpatient stay historically required (modern LDR use is rare in head/neck)

## LDR Disadvantages
- Radiation safety (inpatient, shielded room)
- Source decay (replacement every 3–4 months)
- Less dose sculpting
- Source displacement risk (no afterloading flexibility)

## HDR Advantages
- Outpatient treatment
- Source replacement annually
- Inverse optimization (IPSA / HIPO / MO)
- Steep dose gradient enables OAR sparing
- Reproducible, image-guided daily
- Reasonable cost, workflow efficiency

## HDR Disadvantages
- Radiobiological disadvantage for late tissue (high dose per fraction)
- More sensitive to source position error
- Requires more careful planning
- Multiple daily fractions (6 h spacing)

## PDR (Pulsed Dose Rate)
- Combines LDR radiobiology with HDR technology
- One hourly pulse per hour over 24 h
- 0.5–0.7 Gy/pulse
- Theoretical equivalence to LDR
- Clinical outcomes comparable to LDR in head/neck (limited data)

## Current Standard of Practice
- **Head and neck interstitial**: HDR is now the dominant modality in most centers
- **LDR is used in centers with historical expertise** (e.g., Gustave Roussy, Vienna, Rotterdam)
- **PDR used in selected European centers**
- **Mold therapy (surface)**: HDR is universal; LDR molds largely abandoned

## Radiobiological Conversion
- HDR fractions must be converted using LQ model
- α/β = 10 (tumor), 3 (late)
- EQD2 = D × (d + α/β) / (2 + α/β)
- Example: 5 Gy × 6 fx HDR = 37.5 Gy physical = 50 Gy EQD2 (α/β 10)
- 6 Gy × 5 fx = 30 Gy physical = 50 Gy EQD2 (α/β 10)
- For late tissue: 5 Gy × 6 = 37.5 Gy physical = 65.6 Gy EQD2 (α/β 3)
