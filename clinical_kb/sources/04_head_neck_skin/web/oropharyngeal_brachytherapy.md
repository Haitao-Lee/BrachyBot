# Oropharyngeal and Tonsillar Brachytherapy — Boost and Monotherapy

- **URL**: https://www.brachyjournal.com/article/S1538-4721(20)30100-3/fulltext
- **Document type**: Clinical review
- **Date accessed**: 2026-06-17 (webfetch blocked; content from training-data knowledge)

## Background
Brachytherapy for oropharyngeal cancers (tonsil, base of tongue, soft palate) is technically challenging due to:
- Airway proximity
- Critical OARs (soft palate, mandible, base of skull)
- Need for specialized applicators

## Indications
- T1–T2 tonsillar fossa / soft palate lesions
- Boost to EBRT for T1–T3 (selected)
- Recurrent disease after prior EBRT (selected)
- Boost to IMRT for small-volume residual disease
- Base of tongue (interstitial approach)

## Applicator Options
- **Interstitial catheters** (per-oral or percutaneous) through tonsillar pillar
- **Customized oral molds** with embedded catheters
- **Intracavitary devices** for soft palate lesions
- **3D-printed patient-specific applicators**

## Dose / Fractionation
- **As boost (after 50–60 Gy EBRT)**:
  - 15–20 Gy in 3–5 fractions HDR (5–6 Gy/fx, BID)
  - Total EQD2: 70–80 Gy
- **As monotherapy** (small T1 lesions):
  - 50–60 Gy in 10–15 fractions HDR
- **Recurrent disease (post-EBRT)**:
  - 30–40 Gy in 8–10 fractions HDR
  - Careful OAR dose tracking

## OAR Constraints
| Structure | Constraint (EQD2, α/β 3) |
|---|---|
| Mandible | D0.1cc ≤ 60–70 Gy |
| Spinal cord | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy |
| Brainstem | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy |
| Carotid | D0.1cc ≤ 75 Gy cumulative |
| Soft palate (contralateral) | D0.1cc ≤ 60 Gy |
| Tongue base (opposite) | D0.1cc ≤ 60–65 Gy |
| Pharyngeal constrictors | Mean < 50 Gy (functional swallowing) |
| Larynx | D0.1cc ≤ 60 Gy |

## Outcomes
- Local control: 80–90% (T1–T2, with EBRT)
- Late dysphagia: 10–20%
- Soft-tissue ulceration: 5–10%
- Mandibular ORN: < 5%
- HPV-associated oropharynx has better prognosis; BT may be used in de-escalation trials

## De-escalation Trials (selected)
- ECOG 1308 (HPV+ oropharynx): reduced-dose RT + cetuximab
- NRG HN002 (HPV+ oropharynx): IMRT 60 Gy ± cisplatin
- Future directions: BT boost to reduce total EBRT dose in favorable-risk patients
