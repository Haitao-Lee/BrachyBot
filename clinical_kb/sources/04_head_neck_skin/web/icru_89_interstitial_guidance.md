# ICRU Report 89 — Prescribing, Recording, and Reporting Brachytherapy for Interstitial GYN, H&N, and Other Sites

- **URL**: https://www.icru.org/report/icru-report-89-prescribing-recording-and-reporting-brachytherapy/
- **Document type**: ICRU report (international dosimetry and reporting standard)
- **Date accessed**: 2026-06-17 (webfetch blocked; content from training-data knowledge of ICRU 89)
- **Year**: 2016 (replaces ICRU 58 from 1997)
- **Authors**: International Commission on Radiation Units and Measurements

## Purpose
Updates and modernizes brachytherapy dose reporting:
- Replaces 2D planar reporting (Manchester/Paris) with 3D volumetric
- Standardizes dose metrics: D90, D2cc, D0.1cc
- Defines CTV / GTV / HR-CTV / IR-CTV
- Provides guidance on time-dose patterns, fractionation, BED/EQD2

## Key Concepts

### Volumes
- **GTV (gross tumor volume)**: visible/palpable disease on exam + imaging
- **HR-CTV (high-risk CTV)**: GTV + 5–10 mm margin (modified by anatomy)
- **IR-CTV (intermediate-risk CTV)**: HR-CTV + 5–10 mm (for EBRT integration)
- **OARs**: contoured as solid structures with D2cc and D0.1cc reported

### Dose Metrics
- **D90 (CTV)**: minimum dose covering 90% of CTV
- **D98 (CTV)**: near-minimum dose
- **D2cc (OAR)**: minimum dose in highest 2cc of OAR
- **D0.1cc (OAR)**: minimum dose in highest 0.1cc of OAR (near-maximum)
- **V100 / V150 / V200**: volume receiving 100%/150%/200% of prescription
- **DHI (Dose Homogeneity Index)**: V100 - V150 / V100

### Total Reference Air Kerma (TRAK)
- Sum of air kerma × time products
- Used for inter-implant comparison
- Replaceable by D90 + D2cc reporting

### Time-Dose Pattern
- Multiple fractions per day: 6–8 h spacing
- Total treatment time should be reported
- Effect of treatment time on tumor control (especially for LDR)
- Gap days should be reported and corrected

## Application to Head and Neck BT
- Recommend 3D image-based planning (CT or MRI)
- GTV + 5–10 mm = HR-CTV (modify by anatomy)
- D90 of HR-CTV is the prescription metric
- OAR constraints: D0.1cc (max) and D2cc (avg) reported
- Total dose reported as EQD2 with α/β specified

## Application to Skin BT
- Surface applicator based planning
- Prescription depth: 3–5 mm typical
- V100 of CTV reported
- Bolus: 5–10 mm (for kV X-ray) or 0 (for Ir-192)
- In-vivo dosimetry recommended for first fraction
