## Cosmetic Outcomes
- **Good/excellent cosmesis**: 80–95% of patients
- **Excellent**: flat scar, no contracture, near-skin color
- **Good**: minimal telangiectasia, slight hypopigmentation
- **Factors associated with worse cosmesis**:
  - Larger lesion size (> 3 cm)
  - Electron beam or EBRT (vs BT)
  - Higher dose per fraction (> 5 Gy)
  - Total dose > 60 Gy
  - Location: ear, ala nasi
  - Repeated treatments

## Late Toxicity (RTOG / CTCAE Late)
- Grade 1 (mild): skin atrophy, telangiectasia, hypopigmentation (30–50%)
- Grade 2 (moderate): focal ulceration healing with conservative care (5–15%)
- Grade 3 (severe): non-healing ulcer, necrosis, fibrosis (2–8%)
- Grade 4: chondritis, osteoradionecrosis (rare, < 1%)
- Grade 5: very rare

## Comparison vs Other Modalities

### vs Surgery
- BT preserves tissue (no excision)
- Equivalent LC for small lesions
- Better cosmesis in selected sites (nose, ear, eyelid)
- Patient preference may favor BT for function preservation

### vs EBRT
- Shorter treatment course
- Less integral dose to deeper structures
- Better cosmesis (skin-sparing effect)
- Higher LC in some series for NMSC

### vs Electronic Brachytherapy (eBT)
- eBT: shorter fractions, no bunker
- eBT: limited to < 5 mm depth
- Long-term outcomes of eBT still maturing
- Cost-effectiveness depends on volume and reimbursement

## Risk Factors for Recurrence
- Lesion size > 2 cm
- Depth > 5 mm
- Immunosuppression (transplant, HIV)
- Prior radiation
- Morpheaform / infiltrative histology
- Perineural invasion
- High-risk sites: H-zone of face (nose, lip, eyelid, ear, temple)

## Salvage After BT Failure
- Surgery (first-line if feasible)
- Repeat BT in selected cases (with careful dose tracking)
- Topical therapy (5-FU, imiquimod) for superficial recurrences
- Photodynamic therapy for superficial disease
- Hedgehog inhibitors (vismodegib, sonidegib) for BCC
- Cemiplimab (PD-1 inhibitor) for advanced SCC
