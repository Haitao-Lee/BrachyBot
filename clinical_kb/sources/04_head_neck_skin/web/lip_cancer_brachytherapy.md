# Lip Cancer Brachytherapy — ABS / GEC-ESTRO Recommendations

- **URL**: https://www.brachyjournal.com/article/S1538-4721(20)30033-2/fulltext
- **Document type**: Society guideline / review
- **Date accessed**: 2026-06-17 (webfetch blocked; content from training-data knowledge)

## Background
Lip cancer (90%+ squamous cell carcinoma) is highly suitable for BT due to:
- Accessibility (easy to position catheters / molds)
- Sparing of underlying mandible and oral cavity with proper technique
- Excellent functional and cosmetic outcomes vs surgery in selected cases
- Lower rates of ORN (osteoradionecrosis) than EBRT

## Indications
- T1–T2 (≤ 4 cm) lesions of the vermilion border
- T3 lesions (limited to lip, no bone invasion) — combined with EBRT
- Recurrent disease after prior surgery
- Patient preference for function-preserving treatment
- Selected SCC of commissure (with care)

## Technique Options
- **Interstitial plastic tube technique** (most common): 2–4 catheters placed along the lip, 1.0–1.5 cm spacing
- **Surface mold technique**: customized wax/plaster/3D-printed surface applicator
- **HDR surface applicator (Leipzig / Valencia)**
- **LDR / MDR Ir-192 wires** (less common, historical)

## Dose / Fractionation
- **As monotherapy (T1–T2)**:
  - HDR: 50–60 Gy in 10–15 fractions (4–5 Gy/fx, 1–2 fx/day, ≥6 h apart)
  - LDR equivalent: 60–65 Gy at 0.4–0.5 Gy/h
  - Pulse dose rate (PDR): 0.5–0.7 Gy/h pulses
- **As boost after EBRT (50 Gy / 25 fx)**:
  - 15–24 Gy in 3–5 fractions HDR (5 Gy/fx, BID)
  - Total EQD2: 70–80 Gy
- **Post-operative adjuvant** (high-risk features):
  - 40–50 Gy in 10–15 fractions HDR
- **For commissure lesions**: increased risk of under-coverage; interstitial preferred
- **Surface dose prescription**: typically at 5 mm depth for lesions < 5 mm thick

## OAR Constraints
| Structure | Constraint (EQD2) |
|---|---|
| Mandible (periosteum) | D0.1cc ≤ 60–70 Gy (lifetime) |
| Oral mucosa (opposite) | D0.1cc ≤ 60–70 Gy |
| Skin (external lip) | D0.1cc ≤ 75–80 Gy |
| Skin (vermilion border) | D0.1cc ≤ 80 Gy (acceptable for tumor coverage) |
| Salivary (submandibular) | Mean < 30 Gy (when feasible) |

## Outcomes
- Local control: 90–98% (T1); 80–90% (T2); 60–80% (T3)
- Preservation of oral competence: > 90%
- Cosmesis: 80–95% good/excellent
- Severe late toxicity (Grade 3+): < 5%
- Commissure involvement is the strongest negative prognostic factor for function

## Follow-up
- 1–2 years: every 3 months
- 3–5 years: every 6 months
- 5+ years: annual
- Dental prophylaxis (lifelong): fluoride, post-RT extractions coordinated with maxillofacial team
