## Indications for Head and Neck Interstitial BT
- Primary or recurrent mucosal head and neck cancers (T1–T3) of the:
  - Oral tongue
  - Floor of mouth
  - Buccal mucosa
  - Lip (vermilion)
  - Tonsil / soft palate (selected)
  - Skin of head/neck (face, scalp, nose, ear)
- Boost to EBRT (definitive) for primary disease
- Sole modality for early T1–T2 (≤3 cm) lesions with negative nodes
- Salvage of recurrent/persistent disease after prior EBRT (± surgery)

## Dose / Fractionation (HDR or PDR interstitial)
- **As monotherapy** (selected small T1–T2 lesions):
  - 45–50 Gy in 8–10 fractions (HDR) over 5–7 days
  - 50–55 Gy in 15–20 fractions (HDR) for larger volumes
  - LDR equivalent: 60–65 Gy at 0.4–0.5 Gy/h
- **As boost after EBRT (50 Gy / 25 fx EBRT)**:
  - HDR boost: 15–24 Gy in 3–6 fractions (5–6 Gy per fraction BID)
  - PDR boost: 20–35 Gy hourly pulses (0.5–0.7 Gy/h)
  - Total EQD2 (α/β 10): 70–80 Gy
- **Re-irradiation / salvage** (after prior RT):
  - 30–40 Gy in 8–10 fractions HDR (with care for mandible)
  - EQD2 cumulative often 80–100 Gy with prior RT
- **Lip cancer (HDR surface mold or interstitial)**:
  - 50–60 Gy in 10–15 fractions (1–2 fx/day, ≥6 h apart)

## OAR Dose Constraints (Head & Neck)
| Structure | Constraint (EQD2) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mandible | D2cc ≤ 60–70 Gy, D0.1cc ≤ 75 Gy | Risk of osteoradionecrosis |
| Mucosa (oral) | D2cc ≤ 60 Gy (mono); 70 Gy (boost) | |
| Spinal cord | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy cumulative | |
| Brainstem | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy | |
| Carotid artery | D0.1cc ≤ 75–80 Gy | Stroke / blowout risk |
| Skin surface | D0.1cc ≤ 75 Gy | |
| Brachial plexus | D0.1cc ≤ 66 Gy | |
| Parotid | Mean < 26 Gy (EBRT portion) | |

(For HDR, max doses per fraction × number of fractions; convert using linear-quadratic with α/β 3 for late-responding tissue, α/β 10 for tumor.)

## Technique
- Plastic tube technique (afterloading catheters) preferred
- Catheter spacing 10–15 mm
- Paris system principles for LDR (1.0 cm spacing, equal length, equilateral geometry)
- CT-based planning with 3D contouring of GTV/HR-CTV and OARs
- Step-size source: 5 mm
- Active length covers CTV + 1 cm margin
- Image-guided (CT or MRI) verification before each fraction

## Quality / Toxicity Outcomes
- Local control: 80–95% (T1–T2); 60–80% (T3/recurrent)
- Grade 3+ soft-tissue necrosis: 5–15%
- Osteoradionecrosis: 3–10%
- Soft-tissue ulceration: 5–10% (most heal with conservative care)

## Skin Cancer (companion ABS 2018 + 2020)
- 50–55 Gy in 15–20 fractions (HDR surface applicator)
- 40 Gy in 8 fractions (large/flat lesions, equivalent to 50 Gy EQD2)
- 64 Gy in 32 fractions (twice-daily, electron-like)
- Basal cell / SCC < 5 cm with depth < 5 mm
