# Electronic Brachytherapy (eBT) for Skin Cancer — Esteya, Xoft, Intrabeam

- **Also**: Brachytherapy; ASTRO consensus 2019
- **Document type**: Clinical review / society consensus
- **Date accessed**: 2026-06-17 (webfetch blocked; content from training-data knowledge)

## Overview
Electronic brachytherapy (eBT) refers to HDR surface treatment using a small, electronically generated kilovoltage X-ray source (50–70 kVp). It eliminates the need for radioactive isotopes and shielded bunkers.

## Systems Available
- **Esteya (Elekta, formerly Nucletron)**: 69.5 kVp, 1.5 cm applicator
- **Xoft Axxent (iCAD)**: 50 kVp, 10–50 mm applicators
- **Intrabeam (Carl Zeiss)**: 50 kVp, spherical/surface applicators (3–5 cm)
- **Papillon (Ariane)**: contact X-ray (50 kVp), 1.5–2.5 cm applicators (historical, widely used in UK/France for skin and rectal)

## Physics
- X-ray energy: 50–69.5 kVp
- Very shallow depth dose (D50 at 5 mm)
- Treatment time: 1–3 minutes per fraction (high dose rate)
- Surface dose uniformity: 90% across the applicator face

## Indications
- BCC (nodular, superficial types)
- SCC in situ / Bowen disease
- Superficial SCC (depth < 5 mm)
- Selected keloid (post-excision)
- Lentigo maligna (selected)

## Contraindications
- Lesion depth > 5 mm (infiltrative)
- Bone / cartilage invasion
- Morpheaform / infiltrative BCC
- Perineural invasion
- Recurrent disease after EBRT

## Dose / Fractionation
- **Common scheme**: 40 Gy in 8 fractions (2 fx/week)
- **Alternative**: 50 Gy in 10 fractions (5 fx/week)
- **Accelerated**: 36 Gy in 6 fractions (2 fx/week)
- **Prescription**: at applicator surface or 3 mm depth

## Outcomes (multi-institutional)
- Local control at 1 year: 95–99%
- Cosmesis: 90–95% good/excellent
- Grade 2+ toxicity: < 5%
- Cost-effective in high-volume clinics

## ASTRO Consensus (2019)
- eBT is appropriate for appropriately selected patients with NMSC
- Should be performed by trained radiation oncologists
- Standard dosimetry and QA protocols required
- Patient consent should note the lack of 10-yr outcome data compared to Ir-192

## Reimbursement & Practice Patterns
- Reimbursed in US (CPT codes for HDR surface BT)
- Strong in dermatology office-based setting
- Growing adoption in radiation oncology
- Concerns: cost of equipment, limited payer coverage
