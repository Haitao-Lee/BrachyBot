# Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma (NPC) Brachytherapy — Intracavitary Boost

## Background
Nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC) is endemic in East Asia and Southeast Asia, often presenting with locally advanced disease. Intracavitary brachytherapy (ICBT) has historically been used as a boost to EBRT for:
- T1–T2 NPC confined to the nasopharynx
- Persistent or recurrent disease after definitive EBRT
- Surface mucosal dose escalation to the roof of nasopharynx, fossa of Rosenmüller

## Applicator Design
- **Customized nasopharyngeal mold**: individualized acrylic (PMMA) or silicone applicator molded to the nasopharynx
- **Rotterdam / UMC nasopharyngeal applicator** (commercially available): a paired-catheter intracavitary device
- **Endocavitary afterloading catheters** through the nasal cavity into the nasopharynx
- **3D-printed applicators** now being developed for patient-specific anatomy
- **Hong Kong / Queen Mary Hospital mold**: 3-channel intracavitary mold

## Dose / Fractionation
- **As boost (after 66–70 Gy EBRT)**:
  - 5–8 Gy × 2–3 fractions (HDR, 1 fx/week), total 10–24 Gy
  - Dose prescribed to mucosal surface (5 mm depth) or HR-CTV
  - Total EQD2: 80–90 Gy
- **For persistent disease** (post-EBRT, positive biopsy):
  - 6–8 Gy × 2–3 fractions HDR, often combined with external beam boost
- **As monotherapy** (rare, for very small recurrent lesions):
  - 30–40 Gy in 5–7 fractions HDR

## Outcomes (Historical)
- Local control: 85–95% for T1–T2 NPC
- Complication rates: low (5–10% mucosal necrosis, rare nasopharyngeal stenosis)
- King et al. (AJCO 2000): 5-yr LC 89% with EBRT + ICBT boost
- Wolden et al. (MSKCC): improved LC in T1/T2 with BT boost
- Levendag et al. (Rotterdam applicator): 5-yr LC 100% in T1, 92% in T2
- Recent IMRT era: IMRT alone achieves excellent LC (>90% T1–T2); role of ICBT as boost is now selective

## Modern Role
- With modern IMRT/VMAT achieving high LC, ICBT is less commonly used as a boost
- Re-irradiation salvage for recurrent NPC remains a key indication
- For persistent disease 6–8 weeks post-EBRT with positive biopsy
- GEC-ESTRO/ESTRO acknowledges ICBT as a "valuable option" for selected cases

## OAR Constraints (HDR ICBT for NPC)
| Structure | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Spinal cord / brainstem | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy (cumulative with EBRT) |
| Optic chiasm | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy |
| Lens | D0.1cc ≤ 10 Gy |
| Retina | D0.1cc ≤ 45 Gy |
| Carotid artery | D0.1cc ≤ 75–80 Gy |
| Mucosa (lateral nasopharynx) | D0.1cc ≤ 80–90 Gy cumulative |
| Temporal lobe | D0.1cc ≤ 65 Gy (cumulative) |
| Parotid (mean) | < 26 Gy (EBRT portion) |
