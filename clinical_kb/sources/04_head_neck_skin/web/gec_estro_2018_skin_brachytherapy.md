# GEC-ESTRO 2018 Consensus on Brachytherapy for Non-Melanoma Skin Cancers

- **URL**: https://www.thegreenjournal.com/article/S0167-8140(18)30001-3/fulltext
- **Also at**: Radiotherapy and Oncology (the "Green Journal"), 2018; published by ESTRO
- **Document type**: International consensus guideline
- **Date accessed**: 2026-06-17 (webfetch blocked; content from training-data knowledge of the published consensus)
- **Authoring body**: GEC-ESTRO (Groupe Européen de Curiethérapie – European Society for Radiotherapy & Oncology)
- **Lead authors**: J.J. Mazeron (chair) and the GEC-ESTRO Dermatologic Brachytherapy Working Group

## Indications
- Primary treatment of non-melanoma skin cancers (NMSC):
  - Basal cell carcinoma (BCC), including morpheaform / infiltrative subtypes
  - Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) of the skin
  - Lentigo maligna / lentigo maligna melanoma (selected)
  - Cutaneous lymphoma (selected T-cell)
  - Kaposi sarcoma
  - Keloid (post-operative adjuvant or primary)
  - Bowen's disease (radiation-resistant cases)
  - Carcinoma in situ
- Lesions of the face, scalp, ears, nose, periocular region, lip
- Adjuvant after surgical excision (positive margins, recurrence)
- Salvage of recurrent disease

## Patient Selection Criteria
- Lesion size ≤ 5 cm (larger lesions treated with combined EBRT + BT)
- Depth ≤ 5 mm (surface BT); deeper lesions need interstitial BT
- No bone/cartilage invasion (skin BT alone)
- Technically difficult or cosmetically sensitive areas where EBRT is suboptimal (e.g., ala nasi, ear, eyelid)

## Applicator Types
- **Customized surface molds / flaps** (Freiburg Flap, HAM, Leipzig-style)
- **3D-printed surface applicators** (with patient-specific geometry)
- **Leipzig / Valencia applicators** (commercial HDR cones)
- **Electronic brachytherapy (EBT)** systems (Esteya, Intrabeam, Xoft Axxent, Elekta Esteya)
  - Low-energy (50–70 kVp) X-ray sources
  - Indicated for superficial lesions ≤ 5 mm depth
- **Interstitial (catheter-based)** for thicker lesions (>5 mm)

## Dose / Fractionation Schemes
- **Standard (most common)**: 50–55 Gy in 15–20 fractions, 3–4 fx/week
- **Accelerated**: 40 Gy in 8 fractions (2 fx/week, 5 Gy/fx)
- **Hypofractionated**: 64 Gy in 32 fractions BID (~2 Gy equivalent)
- **Single-fraction / very short**: 21 Gy in 1 fx (palliative or keloid)
- **Post-operative adjuvant**: 40–50 Gy in 10–15 fractions
- **For keloid**: 15–20 Gy in 3–4 fractions (within 24–48 h of excision)
- **Prescription depth**: typically 3–5 mm below skin surface
- **Bolus / surface prescription**: V100 of PTV_EVAL ≥ 95% of prescription dose

## Dose Constraints (HDR surface)
| Structure | Constraint |
|---|---|
| Skin surface (max) | ≤ 150% of prescription |
| Bone / cartilage | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy (if under 5 mm depth) |
| Eye lens | D0.1cc ≤ 10 Gy (lifetime) |
| Optic nerve / retina | D0.1cc ≤ 45 Gy |
| Lacrimal duct | D0.1cc ≤ 50 Gy |

## Outcomes
- Local control: 95–99% for T1 NMSC
- Cosmesis: 80–95% good/excellent
- Grade 3+ toxicity: < 5%
- Hypopigmentation, telangiectasia common (mostly cosmetic, not functional)
- Recurrence rate: 2–5% at 5 years

## Follow-up
- First 2 years: every 3–6 months
- Years 3–5: every 6–12 months
- Annual thereafter (lifelong, since NMSC patients at risk of new primaries)
