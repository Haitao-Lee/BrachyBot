# Brachytherapy for Keloid Scars and Superficial Benign Lesions

## Background
Keloids are benign fibroproliferative scars that extend beyond the original wound. Adjuvant RT after surgical excision is the most evidence-based method to prevent recurrence.

## Mechanism
- Radiation-induced suppression of fibroblast proliferation
- Inhibits neovascularization in the wound bed
- Best effect when delivered within 24–48 h of excision (before proliferative phase)

## Techniques
- **Interstitial (afterloaded catheters)**: 1–2 catheters in the excision bed
- **Surface electron beam or orthovoltage X-ray** (historical)
- **HDR surface mold / flap** (modern): custom applicator placed on the closed wound
- **Intraoperative BT (IOBT)**: catheters placed at surgery, treated post-op day 1–2

## Dose / Fractionation
- **Common scheme**: 15–20 Gy in 3–4 fractions (5 Gy/fx, daily)
  - Initiated 24–48 h post-op
  - Total treatment time < 4 days
- **Single-fraction**: 8–10 Gy intra-op (less common, less data)
- **LDR equivalent**: 20 Gy at 0.4–0.5 Gy/h
- **HDR alternative**: 18 Gy in 3 fractions, every other day

## Outcomes
- Recurrence rate: 10–25% (vs 50–80% with surgery alone)
- Long-term control (5 yr): 75–90%
- Patient satisfaction: > 85%
- Cosmesis: superior to EBRT (less skin discoloration)

## Contraindications
- Recurrent keloid on previously irradiated site
- Pregnancy
- Connective tissue disorders (Ehlers-Danlos) — relative
- Pediatric patients (concerns about growth, malignancy induction)

## Other Superficial Benign Lesions (selected use of BT)
- **Desmoid tumors / aggressive fibromatosis**: adjuvant BT after resection
- **Pterygium**: post-excision β-irradiation (Sr-90) — historical, replaced by anti-VEGF
- **Hemangioma**: rarely used, may cause growth disturbance in children
- **Verrucous carcinoma**: BT as alternative to surgery
- **Bowen's disease (EIS)**: HDR surface
- **Lentigo maligna**: superficial HDR or contact X-ray
