## Clinical Workflow: Tongue BT Implant (HDR)

### 1. Pre-Procedure Evaluation
- Clinical examination with bimanual palpation
- MRI (preferred) or contrast-enhanced CT for T staging
- PET-CT to rule out nodal/distant disease
- Dental evaluation (mandatory pre-RT)
- Nutritional assessment, speech/swallow baseline
- Informed consent (technique, acute and late toxicities)

### 2. Anesthesia & Positioning
- General anesthesia (preferred for tongue) OR awake intubation for selected cases
- Supine position, neck extended
- Nasal intubation to allow tongue protrusion
- Traction suture (e.g., 2-0 silk) at tongue tip for mobilization

### 3. Catheter Placement (Plastic Tube Technique)
- Use of afterloading plastic tubes (e.g., 6F flexible catheters)
- Stainless steel trocars / needles for placement
- Target: 1.0–1.5 cm spacing between catheters
- Typically 3–7 catheters depending on lesion size
- Active (treatment) length = clinical target + 1 cm margin
- Catheter buttons / fixation at skin (catheter exit)
- Verify geometry: parallel catheters, equidistant

### 4. Imaging
- CT simulation with 2–3 mm slice thickness
- Metallic marker wires placed in each catheter
- Image transfer to treatment planning system
- Alternatively: MRI simulation (preferred for soft-tissue tumor definition)

### 5. Contouring
- **GTV**: gross tumor on clinical + imaging
- **CTV / HR-CTV**: GTV + 5–10 mm (modified for anatomy)
- **OARs**: mandible, mucosa (opposite side), skin, tongue base, salivary
- **PRV margins** as appropriate

### 6. Planning
- Inverse optimization (IPSA, HIPO)
- 3 mm or 5 mm step size
- Prescription: D90 of CTV (typically 5–6 Gy/fx)
- OAR constraints applied
- Plan evaluation: D90, D2cc, V100, V150, V200, conformity, homogeneity
- Graphical isodose review
- Independent check by second medical physicist

### 7. Treatment
- 2 fractions per day, ≥ 6 h apart
- HDR Ir-192 source, 10 Ci typical
- Source dwell positions and times per plan
- Daily CT/MRI verification if needed
- In-vivo dosimetry (MOSFET) optional for first fraction

### 8. Catheter Removal
- After final fraction
- All catheters removed as a unit
- Local care of exit sites
- Discharge home same day or next

### 9. Follow-up
- Clinical: 4–6 weeks, then 3 months for 2 years
- Imaging: MRI or CT at 3 months, then as clinically indicated
- Dental: lifelong fluoride, follow-up with prosthodontist
- Speech/swallow: baseline and PRN SLP referral
- Endocrine: TSH at 1 year if neck irradiated

## Specifics: Floor of Mouth
- Catheters placed via submental approach
- Avoid perforation of oral mucosa (keeps catheters submucosal)
- 0.5–1.0 cm spacing
- Single-plane or 2-plane (loop) implants
- Care to spare mandible (D0.1cc < 60 Gy EQD2)

## Specifics: Buccal Mucosa
- Catheters on outer buccal surface
- Spacing 1.0–1.5 cm
- Single plane generally
- Sparing of parotid duct and opposite mucosa
