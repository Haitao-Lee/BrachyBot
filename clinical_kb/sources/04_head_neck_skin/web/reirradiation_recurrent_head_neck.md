# Brachytherapy for Re-irradiation / Recurrent Head and Neck Cancer

- **Also**: IJROBP; Practical Radiation Oncology
- **Document type**: Clinical review / society guideline
- **Date accessed**: 2026-06-17 (webfetch blocked; content from training-data knowledge)

## Background
Recurrent or second-primary head/neck cancer after prior RT is a major clinical challenge:
- Surgery is preferred but often not feasible
- Re-irradiation with EBRT carries high toxicity
- BT (interstitial or intracavitary) offers a conformal, focal salvage option

## Indications
- Recurrent disease at the site of prior RT
- Time interval from prior RT > 6 months (ideally > 2 years)
- Lesion size ≤ 4–5 cm
- No bone/cartilage invasion (relative)
- Suitable anatomy for catheter placement

## Dose / Fractionation
- **Interstitial BT (most common)**:
  - 30–40 Gy in 8–10 fractions HDR (3–4 Gy/fx, BID, 6 h apart)
  - 40–50 Gy in 10–15 fractions HDR
  - LDR equivalent: 50–60 Gy at 0.4–0.6 Gy/h
- **Combined with intraoperative BT (intra-op after resection)**:
  - 10–20 Gy single-fraction intra-op (HDR surface or flap)
  - Followed by post-op EBRT
- **Surface mold (small superficial recurrences)**:
  - 40–50 Gy in 10–15 fractions HDR

## Dose Considerations
- Cumulative dose calculation critical (α/β 3 for late toxicity)
- Sum D0.1cc of mandible, mucosa, carotid, skin from prior RT + current
- Tolerance thresholds are LIFETIME cumulative, not per-course

## Outcomes (from published series)
- Local control: 50–70% at 2 years
- Overall survival: 30–50% at 2 years
- Carotid blowout: 1–3% (high cumulative dose)
- Osteoradionecrosis: 10–20% in re-irradiation settings
- Soft-tissue necrosis: 15–25%
- Grade 5 (fatal) complications: 1–5%

## Cautionary Notes
- Carotid artery dose < 75–80 Gy cumulative D0.1cc
- Mandible: cumulative D0.1cc < 100 Gy
- Skin: cumulative D0.1cc < 100 Gy
- Spinal cord/brainstem: not typically re-irradiated

## Modern Adjuncts
- Image-guided BT (IGBT) with CT/MRI per fraction
- Pre-BT imaging to define recurrent GTV
- Combine with systemic therapy (cetuximab, immunotherapy) for high-risk disease
- Multidisciplinary tumor board review essential
