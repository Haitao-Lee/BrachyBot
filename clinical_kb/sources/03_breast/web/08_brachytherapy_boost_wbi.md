## Background
The brachytherapy boost is used in selected patients (typically < 50 years, high-grade, close/positive margins, extensive DCIS) after whole breast irradiation to escalate dose to the tumor bed, reducing local recurrence risk.

## EORTC 22881-10882 "Boost Trial" (Bartelink et al.)
- 5318 women enrolled
- All received WBI 50 Gy / 25 fractions
- Randomized to boost (16 Gy / 8 fx electrons) vs no boost
- 10-year IBTR: 6.4% (boost) vs 7.8% (no boost) — absolute 1.4% benefit
- Boost benefit greatest in age < 50 (5.8% absolute reduction at 10 y)
- 20-year follow-up: 12.0% IBTR (boost) vs 16.4% (no boost) in age < 50

## Brachytherapy Boost Fractionation Schedules (ABS 2013)

### HDR Boost
- 10 Gy / 2 fractions (1 day apart or 1 week apart)
- 10-12.5 Gy / 4-5 fractions
- 15-16 Gy / 4-6 fractions
- Boost delivered before WBI, during WBI (interstitial catheters in place), or after WBI
- Ir-192 HDR source, 10-12 Ci

### LDR/PDR Boost
- 15-20 Gy continuous (LDR, 0.5 Gy/h)
- 20 Gy PDR in 0.5-1.0 Gy pulses
- Historical; less common in modern practice

## Boost Volume Definition
- CTV_boost = tumor bed + 1-1.5 cm margin (post-surgery)
- Often defined using surgical clips placed at lumpectomy
- 3-5 mm expansion to PTV
- Multi-catheter interstitial or balloon applicator used

## Dose Constraints (Boost)
- CTV_boost V100 ≥ 95%
- D90 ≈ 100% prescription
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Lung V10 ≤ 10% (when feasible)
- Heart Dmean < 2 Gy (left-sided)

## Indication for Boost (Per ASTRO/ESTRO 5th European Breast Cancer Conference)
- Age < 50 years
- High-grade tumors (G3)
- Close or positive margins
- Extensive intraductal component
- Triple-negative or HER2+ tumors (relative)
- Lymphovascular invasion (relative)
- Multifocal disease (relative)

## Comparison of Boost Modalities
- HDR interstitial: highest dosimetric flexibility, best for irregular cavities
- Balloon boost: simple, but limited to spherical cavities
- Electron boost: convenient, but greater skin dose
- Photon boost (3D-CRT): acceptable when BT not available

## Cosmesis with BT Boost
- Interstitial BT boost: similar cosmesis to electron boost
- Balloon boost: slightly worse cosmesis due to device-specific factors
- Fat necrosis rate: 3-5% with interstitial; < 2% with balloon
- Telangiectasia: < 5% with interstitial

## Key Findings
- BT boost is equivalent to electron boost in local control
- Multi-catheter interstitial BT is preferred for irregular cavities
- Balloon BT boost is acceptable for spherical cavities
- 10 Gy / 2 fractions is a common schedule
- Boost use declining with hypofractionated WBI (40 Gy / 15 fx)
- For patients receiving hypofractionated WBI, BT boost may be omitted or reduced
