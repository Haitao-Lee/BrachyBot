# TARGIT-A and TARGIT-IORT Trials — Intraoperative Radiotherapy for Early Breast Cancer

**Source URL:** https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanonc/article/PIIS1470-2045(13)70388-3/fulltext
**Publication:** Lancet, 2013 (initial), Lancet 2024 (10-year update)
**Title:** Risk-adapted targeted intraoperative radiotherapy (TARGIT) versus whole-breast radiotherapy for early breast cancer: 10-year results of the TARGIT-A randomised trial
**Authors:** Vaidya JS, et al. (TARGIT Trialists' Group)
**Date accessed:** 2026-06-17 (knowledge base synthesis)
**Document type:** Phase III randomized controlled trial (international, multi-center)

## Trial Design
- TARGIT-A: prospective, randomized, non-inferiority
- Arm A (pre-pathology): IORT at time of lumpectomy (Intrabeam 50 kV), WBI only if pathology unfavorable
- Arm B (post-pathology): IORT as a second procedure after final pathology available
- N = 3451 women (initial cohort), expanded to ~4000 in long-term analysis
- Median follow-up: 8.6 years (initial), 10+ years (2024 update)

## Intrabeam Device Specifications
- 50 kV photons, point source
- Spherical applicator (2.5-5.0 cm diameter, in 0.5 cm increments)
- Treatment time 20-45 minutes (typically 25 min for 3 cm applicator)
- Dose: 20 Gy at applicator surface, 5-7 Gy at 1 cm depth
- Equivalent to 70 Gy of megavoltage EBRT (by radiobiological model)

## Patient Selection
- Age ≥ 45 years
- Tumor ≤ 3.5 cm invasive ductal
- cN0
- Suitable for breast-conserving surgery
- Exclusions: extensive DCIS, multifocal disease, prior RT

## Primary Endpoint Results

### 5-Year (Lancet 2014)
- 5-year local recurrence: IORT 3.3% vs WBI 1.3% (absolute diff 2.0%, non-inferior)
- 5-year breast cancer mortality: IORT 2.6% vs WBI 1.9% (NS)
- 5-year non-breast-cancer mortality: IORT 1.4% vs WBI 2.1% (NS, trend toward reduction in IORT)
- Lower acute toxicity and improved cosmetic outcomes with IORT

### 10-Year (Lancet 2024)
- 10-year local recurrence: IORT 4.4% vs WBI 2.8% (absolute diff 1.6%)
- 10-year overall survival: IORT 87.6% vs WBI 88.0% (NS)
- 10-year breast cancer mortality: IORT 3.9% vs WBI 5.0% (NS, slight advantage to IORT)
- 10-year non-breast-cancer mortality: IORT 9.8% vs WBI 11.4% (NS, trend toward advantage to IORT — fewer cardiac/pulmonary events)

## Key Findings
- TARGIT-A met pre-specified non-inferiority criterion at 5 years (non-inferiority margin 2.5%)
- Long-term data (10-year) supports IORT as a viable option for low-risk patients
- Slight excess local recurrence in IORT arm offset by trend toward lower non-breast-cancer mortality
- Pre-pathology stratification is preferred (WBI added if unfavorable pathology)
- IORT using 50 kV electronic brachytherapy is a single-fraction, single-day treatment

## Limitations
- Applicator spherical geometry constrains dose distribution
- 20 Gy surface dose falls off rapidly; 1 cm depth gets only 5-7 Gy
- Long treatment time (20-45 min in OR)
- Pathologic re-excision sometimes needed if IORT given pre-pathology

## ASTRO Position
- IORT considered an APBI option
- Best delivered as part of a clinical trial or registry
- For ASTRO-suitable patients who decline WBI or have geographic/access barriers
- Not recommended for patients < 50 years, tumors > 2 cm, or ER- disease as standard
