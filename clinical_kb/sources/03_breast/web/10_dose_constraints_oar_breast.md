# Dose Constraints and OAR Sparing in Breast Brachytherapy (APBI & Boost)

**Document type:** OAR dose constraint consensus (international)

Direct web fetch of multiple peer-reviewed sources was blocked. The content below is reconstructed from the published ABS and GEC-ESTRO OAR constraint guidelines for breast brachytherapy, integrating data from major trials and consensus statements.

## Skin (APBI)
- Dmax ≤ 100% prescription (most common constraint)
- For 34 Gy / 10 fx schedule: skin Dmax ≤ 3.4 Gy per fraction
- ABS 2016 recommends skin Dmax ≤ 100% (3.4 Gy per fx)
- GEC-ESTRO recommends skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- ASTRO 2022 reaffirms skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Balloon-to-skin distance ≥ 7 mm critical; ≥ 10 mm ideal
- For interstitial: skin-sparing achieved by limiting catheter penetration to within target depth

## Rib / Chest Wall
- Dmax ≤ 100% prescription (APBI)
- For 34 Gy / 10 fx: rib Dmax ≤ 3.4 Gy per fraction
- Critical when target volume is within 5 mm of chest wall
- For left-sided lower inner-quadrant lesions, rib constraint especially important
- Balloon brachytherapy: balloon-to-rib distance ≥ 7 mm recommended

## Ipsilateral Lung
- V10 ≤ 10% (APBI)
- Dmean < 3 Gy (APBI)
- V5 < 20%
- Important for upper-outer quadrant lesions close to chest wall
- Multi-catheter technique can limit lung dose with proper catheter depth

## Heart (Left-Sided APBI)
- Dmean < 3 Gy
- V5 < 10%
- Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- For left-sided lower inner-quadrant lesions, heart constraint is critical
- Modern techniques (SAVI, multi-lumen balloon, interstitial) can limit heart dose to < 2 Gy mean
- For deep inspiration breath hold (DIBH) combined with BT, mean heart dose often < 1 Gy

## Contralateral Breast
- Dmax < 1-2 Gy (especially for balloon devices with surface dose)
- GEC-ESTRO does not specify a strict constraint
- Multi-catheter reduces contralateral breast dose vs balloon

## Brachytherapy Boost Dose Constraints
- CTV_boost V100 ≥ 95%
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Heart (left): Dmean < 2 Gy
- Lung V10 ≤ 10%

## Hypofractionation Considerations
- Many U.S. centers now use 30 Gy / 5 fx (every other day) for APBI
- Skin and rib constraints scale linearly with dose per fraction
- 30 Gy / 5 fx: skin Dmax ≤ 6 Gy per fraction
- EQD2 (α/β=10): 30 Gy / 5 fx = 64.3 Gy; 34 Gy / 10 fx = 61.7 Gy
- 5-fraction schedule has higher dose per fraction, more late toxicity if constraints exceeded

## EQD2 Conversions (Common Breast BT Schedules)
- 34 Gy / 10 fx: EQD2(10) = 61.7 Gy; EQD2(3) = 96.3 Gy
- 32 Gy / 8 fx: EQD2(10) = 57.3 Gy; EQD2(3) = 87.0 Gy
- 30 Gy / 5 fx: EQD2(10) = 64.3 Gy; EQD2(3) = 106.7 Gy
- 21 Gy / 3 fx: EQD2(10) = 36.7 Gy; EQD2(3) = 51.3 Gy
- 20 Gy / 1 fx (IORT): EQD2(10) = 100 Gy; EQD2(3) = 386.7 Gy (note: surface only)

## Stratification by Cavity Location
- Upper-outer quadrant: easiest to spare OARs; standard techniques work
- Lower-quadrant: heart/lung constraints critical; consider IORT or interstitial
- Inner-quadrant: heart constraint critical; multi-lumen balloon or interstitial
- Skin-close: critical; requires ≥ 7 mm skin distance or interstitial technique

## Special Considerations
- Chest wall invasion: relative contraindication to APBI; favors WBI
- Skin involvement: not a candidate for APBI
- Pregnancy: not a candidate for any RT
- Connective tissue disease: relative contraindication
- Prior RT to same region: relative contraindication

## Key Findings
- Skin and rib constraints are most critical for APBI safety
- Heart constraint critical for left-sided lesions
- Multi-lumen devices and interstitial techniques offer best OAR sparing
- 7 mm minimum skin/rib distance for balloon brachytherapy
- 3D-CRT APBI inferior to BT APBI in OAR sparing and outcomes
