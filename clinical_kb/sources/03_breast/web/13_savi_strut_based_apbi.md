## Device Description

### SAVI (Strut-Adjusted Volume Implant)
- Multi-catheter device (typically 6-8-10 struts/catheters)
- Peripheral, central, and interstitial struts in single device
- Catheters arranged in a "bunch" geometry
- Diameter 2.5-3.5 cm (collapsed), expands to 3-5 cm cavity
- Allows asymmetric dose modulation (peripheral struts can be loaded or unloaded)
- FDA-approved 2006

### ClearPath (North American Scientific)
- Hybrid design: 4 outer + 1 central catheter
- Spherical geometry, similar to SAVI
- Used in Europe/Asia more than U.S.

### Contura Multi-Lumen Balloon (MLB)
- Spherical balloon with 4-5 lumens (one vacuum port)
- Asymmetric loading possible
- Considered alongside SAVI as "next generation" of balloon devices

### Best Medical International Hybrid
- Multi-lumen balloon-catheter hybrid
- Asymmetric loading with multiple dwell positions

## Dose/Fractionation Schedules
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID at 1 cm from device surface (most common)
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID
- 30 Gy / 5 fractions (every other day)

## Patient Selection
- Cavity size 2.5-4.5 cm (smaller cavity requirement than interstitial)
- Skin distance ≥ 5 mm (slightly more permissive than balloon)
- Better suited for asymmetric cavities (e.g., inner-quadrant lesions near skin/rib)
- ASTRO suitable/cautious criteria apply

## Dosimetric Advantages vs Single-Lumen Balloon
- Asymmetric loading → better skin/rib sparing
- Higher dose modulation in specific regions
- Smaller cavity requirement
- Allows loading in cavity-side while sparing skin-side
- DHI typically 0.7-0.8 (vs 0.6 for single-lumen balloon)

## Clinical Outcomes (SAVI Registry, 5-year data)
- 5-year IBTR: ~2.5% (similar to interstitial)
- 5-year cosmesis "good/excellent": 90%
- Late grade 3 toxicity: < 3%
- Fat necrosis: 3-4% (symptomatic)
- Telangiectasia: < 3%
- Seroma: 10-15% (often asymptomatic)

## Comparison with Multi-Catheter Interstitial
- SAVI requires less specialized training than interstitial
- Equivalent or slightly inferior dosimetric flexibility
- Comparable clinical outcomes in published series
- Easier insertion for community-based centers

## Dose Constraints
- PTV_EVAL V95 ≥ 95%
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- DHI > 0.7
- V150 < 50%; V200 < 20%
- Lung V10 ≤ 10%
- Heart (left): Dmean < 3 Gy

## Technical Considerations
- Device must be expanded fully to contact cavity walls
- Strut positions verified on CT
- Asymmetric loading: keep peripheral struts near skin/rib unloaded or low-weighted
- Loading patterns individually optimized using IPSA or graphical optimization
- Quality assurance: source position check, dwell time verification

## Key Findings
- Strut-based devices offer best dosimetric flexibility among non-interstitial options
- 5-year outcomes comparable to multi-catheter interstitial
- Useful for asymmetric cavities or lesions close to skin/rib
- 34 Gy / 10 fx BID is standard schedule
- SAVI / multi-lumen balloon devices are increasingly used in U.S. APBI practice
