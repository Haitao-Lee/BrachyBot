## Technique Overview
Interstitial multi-catheter brachytherapy is the historical gold standard for APBI, with the longest follow-up of all APBI techniques and the strongest evidence base (GEC-ESTRO phase III).

## Catheter Implantation
- Plastic tubes (5-6 Fr) or rigid stainless steel needles
- Templates used to maintain parallel geometry
- Typically 2-3 planes of catheters for adequate target coverage
- Catheter spacing: 1.0-1.5 cm (typically 1.2-1.5 cm)
- Number of catheters: 10-20 typical (depending on target volume)
- Implantation under local anesthesia with sedation; ultrasound or stereotactic guidance

## Target Definition
- CTV = lumpectomy cavity (visible on CT) + 1-1.5 cm margin
- Limited to 5 mm from skin surface (cropped)
- Limited at chest wall/pectoralis interface (not cropped against muscle)
- PTV_EVAL = CTV (with skin/chest wall cropping per GEC-ESTRO 2018) — note: NSABP uses different cropping rules

## Treatment Planning

### Dose/Fractionation
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID (GEC-ESTRO standard)
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID (NSABP B-39 standard, U.S.)
- 30 Gy / 5 fractions daily, every other day (some U.S. centers)
- 38.5 Gy / 10 fractions BID (Hungarian series)
- HDR (Ir-192) typically 6-12 Ci
- PDR (Ir-192, 0.5-1.0 Gy/pulse) acceptable for delayed/continuous schedules
- LDR (Ir-192 seeds or wires) historical; rarely used now

### Optimization
- Geometric optimization: equal dwell time across all catheters
- Dose point optimization: target dose points around CTV
- Graphical optimization (e.g., HIPO, IPSA): adjust dwell weights interactively
- Inverse planning (IPSA): automated, generates constraints-based plan

## Dose Constraints
- PTV_EVAL V100 ≥ 95%
- PTV_EVAL D90 ≥ 100% prescription
- V150 < 50% (interstitial)
- V200 < 20% (interstitial)
- DHI (Dose Homogeneity Index) > 0.6-0.7
- COIN (Conformity Index) > 0.65
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Chest wall/rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Lung: Dmean < 3 Gy; V5 < 20%
- Heart (left-sided): Dmean < 2 Gy

## Quality Assurance
- Pre-treatment: source strength verification, dwell position check
- During treatment: verify source transit and dwell time
- Catheter reconstruction: ≥ 2 orthogonal films or CT-based
- CT-based 3D planning now standard (replaced 2D planning)
- Real-time plan check before each fraction

## Outcomes (GEC-ESTRO Phase III 5/10-Year)
- 5-year IBTR: 0.92% (interstitial arm) vs 1.05% (WBI)
- 10-year IBTR: 3.5% (interstitial arm) vs 3.0% (WBI)
- Cosmetic "good/excellent": 92% at 5 years
- Late grade 3 toxicity: 2% at 5 years
- Fat necrosis: 3-5% (symptomatic)
- Infection: < 2%
- Telangiectasia: < 5% with proper skin spacing

## Advantages of Interstitial Technique
- Most flexibility for complex target shapes
- Can treat large or irregular cavities
- Better skin/rib sparing than balloon (in some geometries)
- Longest evidence base
- Best for cavities that do not conform to balloon geometry

## Disadvantages
- Technically demanding
- Requires specialized operator training
- Patient discomfort during multi-day implant
- Risk of catheter displacement between fractions
- Limited availability outside major cancer centers
