## Background
The ABS 2016 statement formalized dose/fractionation, technique, and patient selection recommendations for interstitial, balloon, and strut-based APBI delivery, complementing the ASTRO 2016 "suitable/cautionary/unsuitable" framework.

## Patient Selection (ABS 2016)

### Acceptable for APBI (any technique)
- Age ≥ 50 years
- Tumor ≤ 3 cm (T1-T2 ≤ 3 cm)
- Negative margins (≥ 2 mm per ASCO)
- ER+ invasive ductal carcinoma
- DCIS: screen-detected, ≤ 3 cm, margin ≥ 3 mm
- Unifocal disease
- No LVI
- No nodal involvement (clinically/pathologically negative nodes)

### Cautionary
- Age < 50
- Pure DCIS > 3 cm or close margin
- Multifocal disease
- ER- or triple-negative breast cancer
- Invasive lobular carcinoma
- BRCA mutation carriers (consider risk)

### Contraindications
- Multicentric disease
- Extensive LVI
- Node-positive disease
- Pregnancy
- Collagen vascular disease (relative)

## Dose/Fractionation Schedules (ABS 2016)

### Multi-catheter Interstitial (gold standard, most evidence)
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID × 5-7 days (NSABP B-39 standard)
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID
- 30 Gy / 5 fractions (every other day)
- 38.5 Gy / 10 fractions BID (Hungarian series)

### Balloon-based (MammoSite, Contura)
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID at 1 cm from balloon surface
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID
- Balloon-to-skin distance must be ≥ 7 mm (preferably ≥ 10 mm) to minimize skin toxicity

### Strut-based (SAVI)
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID
- Multi-lumen design allows asymmetric loading — preferred for skin/rib sparing

## Technique / Quality Requirements
- CT-based 3D planning for all APBI techniques
- Real-time image guidance for catheter insertion (ultrasound, stereotactic)
- CTV = lumpectomy cavity + 1-1.5 cm margin, limited to 5 mm from skin and chest wall
- PTV_EVAL = CTV minus chest wall and 5 mm skin (cropped structure)
- At least 2-3 multi-planar images for catheter reconstruction
- Plan optimization with graphical optimization or inverse planning (IPSA)

## Dose Constraints (ABS 2016)
- PTV V100 ≥ 95%; D90 ≈ 100% prescription
- V150 < 50 cc (interstitial); V200 < 20 cc
- V150 < 50% (balloon, single-lumen); V200 < 20%
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription (typically ≤ 3.4 Gy/fx for 34/10)
- Rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription (when PTV < 7 mm from chest wall)
- Ipsilateral lung V10 ≤ 10% (balloon/strut)
- Heart Dmean < 3 Gy (left-sided)

## Balloon Brachytherapy Specifics
- Single-entry MammoSite: sphere of 4-5 cm diameter
- 1 cm surface dose prescription
- Balloon-to-skin distance ≥ 7 mm (ideally ≥ 10 mm) to minimize skin toxicity
- Balloon conformity: < 10% asymmetry from ideal sphere
- Hounsfield unit check on planning CT to confirm balloon fills with saline/contrast (not air)

## Key Findings
- Multi-catheter interstitial remains the gold-standard brachytherapy technique with longest follow-up
- Balloon-based devices (MammoSite) have comparable efficacy in selected patients
- Strut-based (SAVI) and multilumen balloon (Contura) allow greater dosimetric flexibility
- ABS endorses ASTRO suitable/cautionary/unsuitable framework
- Brachytherapy boost after WBI: 10 Gy / 2 fx or 10-12.5 Gy / 4-5 fx
- For brachytherapy boost, no consensus on a single fractionation; ABS allows 10-16 Gy in 2-5 fractions
