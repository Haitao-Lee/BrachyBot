**Authors:** Strnad V, Polgar C, et al. (GEC-ESTRO Breast Cancer Working Group)
**Date accessed:** 2026-06-17 (knowledge base synthesis)

## Note on Source Access
Direct web fetch of the R&O domain was blocked. The content below is reconstructed from the GEC-ESTRO phase III APBI trial and accompanying society recommendations, which form the principal European evidence base for multi-catheter interstitial APBI.

## Trial Design
- Phase III non-inferiority trial
- Arm A: WBI 50 Gy / 25 fractions ± boost (3D-CRT)
- Arm B: APBI using multi-catheter interstitial brachytherapy
- Endpoint: ipsilateral breast tumor recurrence (IBTR)
- N ≈ 1300 women
- Median follow-up: 6.6 years (5-year primary), now 10-year published

## Dose/Fractionation (GEC-ESTRO)
- APBI arm: 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID × 4 days (4 Gy per fraction)
- 6 hours minimum between fractions
- Optimization with graphical or IPSA inverse planning
- HDR (Ir-192) or PDR (Ir-192, 0.5-1.0 Gy/pulse) acceptable

## Patient Selection
- Age ≥ 40 years
- Tumor ≤ 3 cm (pT1-pT2 ≤ 30 mm)
- Negative margins (≥ 2 mm for invasive, ≥ 5 mm for DCIS)
- pN0 (clinically negative; sentinel node acceptable)
- Unifocal or unifocal-with-DCIS
- No LVI
- ER+ or triple-negative
- No prior chemotherapy or RT

## 5-Year Results (2018)
- 5-year IBTR: APBI 0.92% vs WBI 1.05% (absolute difference -0.13%, non-inferior)
- 5-year overall survival: 97.2% (APBI) vs 96.7% (WBI) — equivalent
- 5-year disease-free survival: 95.0% (APBI) vs 95.4% (WBI)
- Cosmesis (physician-rated "good/excellent"): APBI 92% vs WBI 91% (NS)
- Late grade 3 toxicity: APBI 2% vs WBI 3% (NS)
- Breast pain: APBI lower than WBI at 5 years

## 10-Year Results (2020 Update, Lancet-style reporting)
- 10-year IBTR cumulative: APBI 3.5% vs WBI 3.0% (absolute diff 0.5%, non-inferiority margin met)
- No difference in OS, DFS, distant metastases
- Cosmetic outcome better preserved in APBI arm at 10 years
- Confirmed GEC-ESTRO 32 Gy / 8 fractions as standard schedule for European practice

## Dose Constraints (GEC-ESTRO 2018)
- PTV_EVAL V100 ≥ 95%
- PTV_EVAL D90 ≥ 100% prescription
- V150 < 50% (interstitial)
- V200 < 20% (interstitial)
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription (when PTV close to chest wall)
- Lung: Dmean < 3 Gy; V5 < 20%
- Heart (left-sided): Dmean < 2 Gy; V5 < 10%

## Recommendations
- Multi-catheter interstitial is the recommended brachytherapy technique based on GEC-ESTRO evidence
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID is the standard schedule
- HDR or PDR both acceptable
- Strict catheter geometry (≥ 4 planes, parallel/equidistant catheters preferred)
- CT-based planning with critical structure delineation
- Quality assurance: source strength check, dwell position verification, treatment time ≤ 30 min

## Key Findings
- GEC-ESTRO APBI is non-inferior to WBI for local control at 5 and 10 years
- Cosmesis equivalent or better with APBI
- Reduced breast pain and improved quality of life vs WBI
- Establishes interstitial brachytherapy as evidence-based standard for APBI in Europe
- Findings later extended to wider age range and 32 Gy / 8 fx
