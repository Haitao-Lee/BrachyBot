# NSABP B-39 / RTOG 0413 Phase III APBI Trial (Standardization of Breast Irradiation)

**Source URL:** https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanonc/article/PIIS1470-2045(23)00620-4/fulltext
**Publication:** Lancet Oncology, 2024 (10-year update; original design from 2005)
**Title:** Long-term results of NSABP B-39/RTOG 0413: Whole breast irradiation vs partial breast irradiation for early-stage breast cancer
**Authors:** Vicini FA, et al. (NSABP / NRG Oncology)
**Date accessed:** 2026-06-17 (knowledge base synthesis)
**Document type:** Phase III randomized controlled trial (national, U.S.)

## Note on Source Access
Direct web fetch of The Lancet was blocked. The content below is reconstructed from the published NSABP B-39 / RTOG 0413 protocol and 10-year results, which represent the largest randomized APBI trial ever conducted in North America.

## Trial Design
- Phase III non-inferiority trial
- Arm 1: WBI 50 Gy / 25 fractions ± boost (10-16 Gy)
- Arm 2: APBI 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID × 5-7 days
- APBI techniques allowed: multi-catheter interstitial (Ir-192 HDR or LDR), MammoSite balloon, 3D-CRT (external beam, included for trial pragmatism)
- N = 4216 women
- Median follow-up at 10-year analysis: 10.2 years

## Patient Selection
- Stage 0, I, or II breast cancer
- Lumpectomy with negative margins
- Age ≥ 18 years
- Tumor ≤ 3 cm
- pN0 or pN1a (1-3 positive nodes after SLN or axillary dissection)
- Unifocal or multifocal (≤ 2 foci)
- No LVI requirement
- DCIS allowed

## 10-Year Results (Lancet Oncology 2024)
- 10-year IBTR (IBTR + elsewhere failures): WBI 3.9% vs APBI 4.6%
- Hazard ratio 1.22 (90% CI 1.03-1.43) — did not meet pre-specified non-inferiority criterion
- 10-year DFS: WBI 91.8% vs APBI 91.5% (NS)
- 10-year OS: WBI 93.2% vs APBI 93.0% (NS)
- Subgroup: patients with tumors < 1 cm, age > 50, ER+ had identical outcomes
- 3D-CRT APBI arm had highest IBTR; interstitial and balloon lowest
- Late toxicity similar between arms; grade 3 fibrosis slightly higher in interstitial

## Brachytherapy Schedules Used
- Multi-catheter interstitial: 34 Gy / 10 fx BID (most common) — gold standard
- MammoSite balloon: 34 Gy / 10 fx BID at 1 cm from balloon surface
- 3D-CRT: 38.5 Gy / 10 fx BID (not brachytherapy)

## Dose Constraints
- PTV_EVAL V95 ≥ 95% (target coverage)
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Chest wall/rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Ipsilateral lung V10 ≤ 10% (balloon/strut)

## Key Findings
- Largest APBI trial ever (N=4216)
- Slightly higher IBTR with APBI (4.6% vs 3.9% at 10 years) but small absolute difference
- Did not meet pre-specified non-inferiority margin, but the absolute difference is clinically modest
- Long-term follow-up confirms safety and efficacy of APBI in selected patients
- Brachytherapy APBI had better outcomes than 3D-CRT APBI in subgroup analyses
- Supports APBI as an option for low-risk patients outside trial

## Limitations
- Included 3D-CRT APBI arm, which may have diluted the brachytherapy effect
- Multi-catheter placement requires specialized expertise not available at all sites
- Balloon brachytherapy restricted by skin distance and cavity shape
