## Devices

### Intrabeam (Carl Zeiss Meditec)
- 50 kV point source X-ray (miniature x-ray tube)
- Spherical applicators: 2.5-5.0 cm in 0.5 cm increments
- Treatment time 20-45 minutes
- Dose: 20 Gy prescribed to applicator surface
- 5-7 Gy at 1 cm depth
- Used in TARGIT-A trial
- Spherical dose distribution

### Xoft Axxent (electronic brachytherapy)
- 50 kV source (similar to Intrabeam)
- Multi-channel balloon or flexible catheter
- Source step size 2.5-5 mm
- Treatment time 8-10 minutes per fraction (HDR-like)
- Used for multiple fractions (typically 1-4 fractions)
- FDA-approved for breast IORT

## Patient Selection (ASTRO Appropriate Use Criteria 2014)
- Age ≥ 60 years (per ACR/ASTRO 2014)
- Invasive ductal carcinoma ≤ 2 cm (updated 2022: ≤ 2 cm for suitable, ≤ 3 cm with caveats)
- Margins ≥ 2 mm
- ER+
- No LVI
- Not BRCA1/2 carrier
- Unifocal
- cN0

## TARGIT Trial Long-Term Results
- 10-year local recurrence: IORT 4.4% vs WBI 2.8% (absolute diff 1.6%, non-inferior)
- 10-year overall survival: equivalent
- Lower non-breast-cancer mortality with IORT (trend)
- ASTRO: IORT is "appropriate" only for low-risk, suitable ASTRO group

## TARGIT-IORT vs TARGIT-WBI Trial (Vaidya 2020)
- Compared IORT to WBI in "prepathology" timing
- IORT arm: 22.2% required additional WBI for unfavorable pathology
- Final IORT-only group had slightly higher local recurrence but met non-inferiority

## Dose/Fractionation
- Single fraction 20 Gy to applicator surface (Intrabeam, TARGIT protocol)
- Single fraction 21 Gy (Xoft)
- Multi-fraction 8-10 Gy × 2-3 fx (Xoft, off-label)
- 50 kV dose falls off rapidly: 1 cm depth gets 25-35% of surface dose

## Dose Distribution Characteristics
- Spherical isodose distribution
- Rapid fall-off: dose at 1 cm = 5-7 Gy for 20 Gy surface
- EQD2 (surface): 20 Gy × 1 = 100 Gy EQD2(10) (using α/β=10)
- EQD2 (1 cm depth): 6 Gy × 1 = 16.7 Gy EQD2(10) — subtherapeutic at depth
- This is why IORT is used only for very low-risk disease

## Comparison with External Beam APBI
- IORT: single dose, intra-operative, no late toxicity data beyond 10 years
- EBRT APBI: 5-10 fractions, 1-2 weeks, mature data
- IORT may have lower skin/heart/lung dose
- IORT requires anesthesia and operating room time

## Limitations
- Limited to T1 lesions with low-risk features
- Cannot treat DCIS effectively (subtherapeutic at depth)
- Cannot treat EIC or multifocal disease
- Cost of equipment and OR time
- Limited 10-year data
- Salvage mastectomy may be technically more difficult after IORT

## Key Findings
- IORT is an APBI option for very select low-risk patients
- TARGIT-A 10-year data supports non-inferiority
- IORT should be offered in trial/registry setting
- 50 kV electronic brachytherapy: rapid fall-off, surface dosing
- Limited applicability; not for general APBI practice
