## NCCN APBI Recommendations (2024)

### "Suitable" for APBI
- Age ≥ 50
- Tumor ≤ 2 cm
- ER+ invasive (or DCIS, low/intermediate grade)
- Margins ≥ 2 mm (invasive), ≥ 3 mm (DCIS)
- No LVI
- Unifocal
- Axillary node-negative (or pN0(i+)/pN1mi with favorable biology)

### "Cautious" for APBI
- Age 40-49 with otherwise suitable features
- Tumor 2-3 cm
- Close margins < 2 mm
- Multifocal disease (≤ 2 foci)
- Micropapillary histology
- EIC limited
- DCIS > 2 cm or close margin

### "Unsuitable" for APBI
- Age < 40
- Tumor > 3 cm
- Positive margins
- Multicentric disease
- Extensive LVI
- Node-positive disease (≥ 4 nodes positive)
- BRCA1/2 mutation carriers (consider genetic counseling, may be suitable if ASTRO criteria met)

## Recommended Fractionation Schedules
NCCN endorses both ASTRO and GEC-ESTRO schedules:
- 34 Gy / 10 fx BID (5-7 days)
- 32 Gy / 8 fx BID (4 days)
- 30 Gy / 5 fx daily, every other day
- 38.5 Gy / 10 fx BID (Hungarian)
- 21 Gy / 1 fx IORT (TARGIT, within trial/registry)
- 20 Gy / 1 fx IORT (Intrabeam, within trial/registry)

## APBI Techniques Listed in NCCN
- Multi-catheter interstitial (HDR/PDR)
- Balloon-based (MammoSite, Contura)
- Strut-based (SAVI)
- IORT (Intrabeam, Xoft — only in trial/registry)
- External beam 3D-CRT (acceptable but inferior to BT in subgroup analyses)

## Brachytherapy Boost Recommendations
- 10-15 Gy in 2-5 fractions
- 10 Gy / 2 fx is a common schedule
- For age < 50, high-grade, close margins
- NCCN allows BT, electron, or photon boost

## Dose Constraints (NCCN endorses ASTRO/GEC-ESTRO)
- PTV_EVAL V95 ≥ 95% (target)
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Chest wall/rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Heart (left): Dmean < 3 Gy
- Ipsilateral lung V10 ≤ 10%

## Clinical Implementation Notes
- NCCN recommends APBI only for women meeting ASTRO 2022 suitable or cautious criteria
- For BRCA1/2 mutation carriers, NCCN recommends against APBI outside clinical trial
- For ER+ DCIS, APBI is a reasonable alternative to WBI
- For triple-negative invasive, APBI is cautiously acceptable but WBI is preferred
- For invasive lobular, APBI is cautiously acceptable

## Key Findings
- NCCN endorses ASTRO 2022 risk-adapted approach
- 10-year data from NSABP B-39 and GEC-ESTRO support APBI safety in selected patients
- IORT (TARGIT) is acceptable only in trial/registry per NCCN 2024
- Brachytherapy boost remains an option for high-risk patients after WBI
- Brachytherapy is preferred over 3D-CRT for APBI based on subgroup analyses
