## Device Description

### MammoSite (single-lumen balloon)
- Spherical balloon, 4-5 cm diameter (10-15 cm³ volume range)
- Single central catheter with single dwell position
- Balloon inflated with saline + 5-10% contrast for CT visualization
- FDA-approved 2002

### MammoSite ML (multi-lumen)
- Spherical balloon, 4-5 cm diameter
- 1 central + 8 peripheral lumens, allowing asymmetric dose modulation
- Enables better skin/rib sparing

### Contura Multi-Lumen Balloon (MLB)
- Spherical balloon, 3-4 cm
- 4-5 dwell positions in vacuum-port lumens
- Allows asymmetric loading
- Slightly different geometry than MammoSite

### Axxent (Xoft Electronic Brachytherapy, non-balloon)
- Balloon + HDR kV electronic source (50 kV, similar to Intrabeam)
- Multichannel balloon catheter
- Shorter treatment time (8-10 min per fraction) than Ir-192 HDR

## Dose/Fractionation Schedules
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID at 1 cm from balloon surface (most common)
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID at 1 cm from balloon surface
- 28 Gy / 7 fractions BID (off-label, some institutions)
- 6 hours between fractions
- Surface of balloon: 100% prescription
- 1 cm from balloon surface (skin-facing side): 100% prescription

## Eligibility / Quality Criteria
- Balloon-to-skin distance ≥ 7 mm (ideally ≥ 10 mm) — critical to avoid skin toxicity
- Balloon-to-rib distance ≥ 7 mm
- Balloon asymmetry: < 10% from ideal sphere (some institutions accept up to 2 mm deviation)
- No air in balloon (Hounsfield units on CT should be uniform)
- Balloon must conform to cavity walls (no fluid gap)
- Cavity size appropriate (3-5 cm diameter typical)

## Dose Constraints
- PTV_EVAL: balloon surface + 1 cm expansion, cropped 5 mm from skin and chest wall
- PTV_EVAL V95 ≥ 95% (or V100 ≥ 95%)
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription (e.g., 3.4 Gy per fraction for 34/10)
- Chest wall/rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Ipsilateral lung V10 ≤ 10%
- Heart (left-sided): Dmean < 3 Gy
- Dose homogeneity index (DHI): (V100 - V150)/V100 > 0.75
- For multi-lumen devices: V150 < 50%; V200 < 20% (more easily met)

## Outcomes

### 5-Year MammoSite Registry
- 5-year IBTR: ~2.5% (1449 patients, multi-institution registry)
- 5-year cosmesis "good/excellent": 80-90%
- Symptomatic seroma: 10-15%
- Telangiectasia, fat necrosis rates < 5% with proper skin spacing
- Infections: 5-10% (managed with antibiotics; rarely require device removal)

### Comparison with Multi-catheter
- Equivalent or slightly higher local recurrence in some studies
- Lower cosmesis in some series due to skin toxicity
- Simpler insertion technique, wider availability

## Key Findings
- Balloon brachytherapy offers a more accessible APBI technique than interstitial
- Multi-lumen balloons (Contura, MammoSite ML) reduce skin/rib toxicity vs single-lumen
- Strict skin distance criteria (≥ 7 mm) essential for safety
- 34 Gy / 10 fx BID is the standard U.S. schedule
