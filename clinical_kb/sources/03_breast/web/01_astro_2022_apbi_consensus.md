## Background
The 2022 ASTRO statement broadens the 2016 "suitable/cautionary/unsuitable" framework and moves to a risk-adapted approach for DCIS and early-stage invasive breast cancer, incorporating 10-year results of several randomized trials (TARGIT, ELIOT, GEC-ESTRO, NSABP B-39/RTOG 0413).

## APBI Patient Selection — 2022 Risk-Adapted Groups

### "Suitable" (low risk of ipsilateral breast tumor recurrence)
- Age ≥ 50 years
- Tumor size ≤ 2 cm (T1)
- ER+ invasive (any PR/HER2 allowed for DCIS)
- Negative surgical margins (≥ 2 mm invasive; per ASCO/CAP for DCIS)
- No LVI
- Unifocal disease
- Pure DCIS: screen-detected, low/intermediate grade, size ≤ 2.5 cm, margin ≥ 3 mm
- Suitable for all APBI techniques (interstitial, balloon, IORT)

### "Cautious" (acceptable but acknowledge gaps in evidence)
- Age 40-49 years (with otherwise suitable features)
- Tumor size 2.1-3.0 cm
- Close margins < 2 mm
- Micropapillary histology
- Limited LVI
- Multifocal disease (≤ 2 foci)
- DCIS ≤ 3 cm, close margin < 3 mm
- EIC (extensive intraductal component) limited

### "Unsuitable" (outside ASTRO APBI recommendations)
- Age < 40 years
- Tumor > 3 cm
- Positive margins
- BRCA1/2 mutation carriers (consider cautiously)
- Multicentric disease
- Extensive LVI
- Invasive lobular with pleomorphic features (relative)
- Node-positive disease

## Recommended Fractionation Schedules (APBI, 2022)

### Multi-catheter Interstitial (most evidence)
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID × 5 days (NSABP B-39/RTOG 0413 standard arm)
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID (GEC-ESTRO protocol)
- 30 Gy / 5 fractions once daily, every other day
- 38.5 Gy / 10 fractions BID (Hungarian)

### Balloon-based (MammoSite, Contura)
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID (most common U.S. regimen)
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID
- Surface dose (1 cm from balloon surface) = prescription

### Strut-based (SAVI, Contura multilumen)
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID
- Allows asymmetric dose modulation to spare skin/rib/chest wall

### Intraoperative (IORT) — only for ASTRO-suitable patients, ideally within a trial or registry
- 20 Gy single-fraction Intrabeam (TARGIT-E)
- 21 Gy single-fraction Xoft Axxent (kV)
- 50 kV photons prescribed to surface of applicator

## Brachytherapy Boost After Whole Breast Irradiation (WBI)
- 10-15 Gy in 2-5 HDR fractions delivered before or after WBI
- Typical sequence: WBI 45-50 Gy / 25 fractions, then BT boost 10 Gy / 2 fx
- Equivalent to 10-16 Gy electron boost
- ASTRO does not specify a single fractionation; GEC-ESTRO recommends 10 Gy in 2 fx for HDR boost

## Dose Constraints (APBI)
- PTV_EVAL: V95 ≥ 95% (target coverage)
- PTV D90 ≈ 100% prescription
- Skin (most superficial 5 mm): Dmax ≤ 100% prescription (≤ 3.4 Gy per fx for 34/10)
- Chest wall/rib: Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Ipsilateral lung: V10 ≤ 10% (when feasible)
- Heart (left-sided): Dmean ≤ 3 Gy; minimize V5

## Key Findings
- ASTRO broadened "suitable" criteria vs 2016 to include women 40-49 with otherwise low-risk features (cautious group)
- 10-year GEC-ESTRO data: APBI 34/10 BID non-inferior to WBI for local control and cosmesis
- 10-year TARGIT: IORT non-inferior to WBI for selected low-risk patients
- 10-year NSABP B-39/RTOG 0413: APBI had slightly higher IBTR than WBI (4.6% vs 3.9% at 10 y) but well within non-inferiority margin
- ASTRO recommends APBI as a standard option for women meeting suitable criteria outside clinical trial
- IORT should be offered only in a trial/registry setting for now
