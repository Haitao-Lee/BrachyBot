## Background
The ESTRO 2018 consensus updated the GEC-ESTRO recommendations on target volume definition for APBI, with a focus on harmonization across the European and American practice.

## Target Volume Definitions

### CTV (Clinical Target Volume) - Tumor Bed Plus Margin
- CTV = lumpectomy cavity (visualized on CT) + 1.5 cm isotropic margin
- Limited to 5 mm from skin surface
- Limited at pectoralis/chest wall (does NOT include muscle unless invasion)
- Limited by anatomic boundaries (e.g., skin, fascia, bone)
- For DCIS: use 2 cm margin around cavity

### PTV (Planning Target Volume)
- CTV + 0.5 cm margin
- PTV must be cropped 5 mm from skin and at chest wall/pectoralis
- PTV_EVAL (used for reporting) = CTV with 5 mm skin/chest wall cropping — this is the volume used for V95, D90

### ITV
- Not typically used in APBI
- For deformable registration between imaging sets, an ITV may be considered

## Margins Recommended

### Cavity Visualization
- Use surgical clips (typically 4-6) placed at cavity edges
- Cavity visible on planning CT as a fluid/seroma cavity
- If cavity not visible (e.g., clips only), use 1-1.5 cm around the surgical bed

### CTV Margin
- 1.5 cm from cavity (ESTRO recommendation)
- 1.0 cm (GEC-ESTRO 2018 alternative)
- For DCIS: 2.0 cm from cavity

## Dose/Fractionation Schedules (ESTRO 2018 Endorsed)
- 32 Gy / 8 fractions BID (4 days) — GEC-ESTRO standard
- 30 Gy / 5 fractions, every other day
- 34 Gy / 10 fractions BID (5-7 days) — NSABP standard
- 38.5 Gy / 10 fractions BID — Hungarian
- 40 Gy / 15 fractions (hypofractionated, off-label for APBI)
- 50 Gy / 25 fractions (rarely used for APBI)

## Dose Constraints (ESTRO 2018)

### Target Coverage
- PTV_EVAL V95 ≥ 95% (target)
- PTV_EVAL D90 ≈ 100% prescription
- D98 ≥ 90% prescription
- D2 ≤ 130% prescription (avoid hot spots)

### Critical Structures
- Skin Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Rib Dmax ≤ 100% prescription
- Heart (left): Dmean < 2 Gy; V5 < 10%
- Lung: Dmean < 3 Gy; V10 ≤ 10%
- Contralateral breast: minimize (Dmax < 1 Gy)

## Cavity vs No Cavity
- Most centers use a cavity-based CTV
- "No cavity" approach (clips only) may be considered for cavities that are difficult to visualize
- In the "no cavity" approach, CTV = surgical bed defined by pre-op imaging + clips + seroma cavity

## Dose Reporting Recommendations
- D90, V100, V95, V150, V200 (PTV_EVAL)
- DHI = (V100 - V150)/V100
- COIN (Conformity Index) = (PTV_EVAL ∩ Rx isodose) / (PTV_EVAL ∪ Rx isodose)
- OAR Dmax, Dmean, Vx for each OAR

## Key Findings
- 1.5 cm margin from cavity is standard CTV definition
- 5 mm skin and chest wall cropping is essential
- ESTRO endorses 32 Gy / 8 fx BID as primary schedule
- Multiple schedules acceptable
- Consistent reporting metrics enable trial comparison
