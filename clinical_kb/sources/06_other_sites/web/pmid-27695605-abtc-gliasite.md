# GliaSite Balloon Brachytherapy (GSBT) — ABTC dose-finding trial

## Source
- PMID: 27695605
- Journal of Radiation Oncology 2015 Sep;4(3):235-241
- Adult Brain Tumor Consortium (ABTC)

## Device
- GliaSite: balloon catheter placed in resection cavity
- Subcutaneous port allows filling with liquid I-125 (Iotrex)
- Radiation dose decreases uniformly with distance from balloon surface

## Methods
- Prospective dose-finding trials
- NEW-GBM: previously untreated, balloon placed before standard RT
- REC-HGG: recurrent high-grade glioma after surgery
- 10 NEW-GBM, 5 REC-HGG enrolled
- Only 2/10 NEW-GBM and 2/5 REC-HGG assessable at 90 days (most progressed before evaluation)

## Outcomes
- Median survival from treatment:
  - NEW-GBM: 15.3 months (95% CI 7.1-23.6)
  - REC-HGG: 12.8 months (95% CI 4.2-20.9)
- No dose-limiting toxicities observed
- Trials closed for poor assessability — early imaging changes confounded interpretation

## Key takeaways
- GliaSite was an approved intracavitary balloon BT system
- Currently largely supplanted by Cs-131 collagen tiles (GammaTile) and other intraoperative modalities
- Demonstrates feasibility of balloon-based intracavitary LDR in GBM
- "MRI treatment effect vs recurrence" remains a challenge for response assessment
