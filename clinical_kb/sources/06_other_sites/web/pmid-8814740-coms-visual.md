# Long-term Visual Outcome in Uveal Melanoma Treated with Plaque Brachytherapy

## Source
- PMID: 8814740
- British Journal of Ophthalmology 1996 Feb;80(2):117-124
- University of California San Francisco

## Methods
- 426 ciliochoroidal melanomas meeting COMS inclusion criteria
- Treated with either helium ions or 125I brachytherapy
- Compared to COMS enucleation vs plaque RCT

## Outcomes
- 36% of eyes had 6/12 or better visual acuity at 3 years
- Visual retention dependent on:
  - Tumor thickness
  - Tumor location (optic disc, fovea, ciliary body)
  - Patient age
  - Pre-operative visual acuity
- 5-year actuarial metastatic rate ~13%
- Risk of visual loss greatest immediately after treatment, then declined

## Mortality association
- Patients at greatest risk of post-radiation visual loss had significantly greater tumor-related mortality

## Key takeaways
- Established that plaque brachytherapy is a viable eye-conserving option
- Visual preservation is achievable in ~1/3 of patients at 3 years
- Documented reference data for COMS design
