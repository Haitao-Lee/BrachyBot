# Vascular Brachytherapy for In-Stent Restenosis (Historical Context)

## Source
- PMID: 12957267
- Cardiovascular Revascularization Medicine 2003

## Background
- 1997-2003: Vascular brachytherapy was FDA-approved for treatment of in-stent restenosis (ISR)
- Two systems commercialized:
  1. Beta emitters: Sr-90/Y-90 (Beta-Cath, Novoste), P-32 (Guidant)
  2. Gamma emitters: Ir-192 (Cordis Checkmate)
- Prescribed dose: 14-18 Gy at 2 mm vessel wall

## Pivotal trials
- START (Sr-90) — 476 patients, 14 Gy at 2 mm
- INHIBIT (P-32) — 332 patients
- GAMMA-1, SCRIPPS, WRIST (Ir-192)

## Outcomes
- Reduced repeat revascularization vs placebo at 6-12 months
- Late thrombosis and edge effect observed
- Late catch-up in some studies

## Decline after drug-eluting stents (DES)
- Sirolimus-eluting (2003) and paclitaxel-eluting (2004) stents dramatically reduced ISR
- Vascular BT use dropped from thousands/year to nearly zero
- Currently reserved for refractory ISR not amenable to repeat DES

## Status
- Rarely used today; not a routine indication
- Sr-90 and P-32 systems mostly withdrawn
- Mentioned for completeness in ABS/GEC-ESTRO historical surveys
