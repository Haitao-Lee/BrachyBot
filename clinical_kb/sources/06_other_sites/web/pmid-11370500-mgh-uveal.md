# Eye Conservation for Uveal Melanoma — Comprehensive Review

## Source
- PMID: 11370500
- Hematology/Oncology Clinics of North America 2001 Apr;15(2):389-402
- Massachusetts General Hospital

## Key findings
- Two main eye-conservation modalities:
  1. External beam charged particle (proton) therapy
  2. Episcleral radionuclide plaque (I-125 most common; also Ru-106, Pd-103)
- Both achieve high local control (75-80% eye salvage for large tumors with protons)
- Survival not compromised compared to enucleation

## Doses / outcomes
- 125I plaque: 85 Gy to tumor apex, 5-7 days continuous
- UCSF-LBL trial: helium ions vs 125I plaque
  - Charged particle therapy superior in local tumor control and eye retention
- 5-year actuarial metastatic rate ~13%

## Quality of life
- Long-term quality of life similar across treatment modalities
- Visual retention depends on tumor thickness, location (optic disc / macula), and patient age
- 36% of eyes had 6/12 or better visual acuity at 3 years

## Treatment indication
- Small-to-medium uveal melanomas (typically up to ~10-12 mm thickness)
- Plaque size selected 4-6 mm larger than tumor base
- 125I most common worldwide; Ru-106 dominant in Europe
