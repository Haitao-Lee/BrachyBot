# High-dose-rate endobronchial brachytherapy (HDREB) — single-institution experience

## Source
- PMID: 37217415
- Journal: Brachytherapy 2023 Jul-Aug;22(4):542-546
- Affiliation: Cancer Centre of Southeastern Ontario, Queen's University, Kingston, Ontario, Canada

## Methods
- Retrospective chart review 2010-2019
- 58 patients with malignant airway disease
- Prescription: 14 Gy in 2 fractions, 1 week apart (most common regimen)
- 84.5% primary lung cancer; 86% stage III/IV
- 52% had prior EBRT; 8 treated in ICU
- 38% had complete airway obstruction

## Outcomes
- Dyspnea improved in 72% (mMRC score drop of 1.13 points, p<0.001)
- Hemoptysis improved in 88%
- Cough improved in 48.6%
- Median progression-free survival: 6.5 months
- Median overall survival: 10 months
- Grade 4-5 events in 13% (median 2.5 months post-BT)

## Key takeaways
- HDREB offers significant symptomatic benefit in advanced malignant airway disease
- 14 Gy in 2 fractions weekly is the institutional standard of care
- Even ICU patients and those with complete obstruction benefit
