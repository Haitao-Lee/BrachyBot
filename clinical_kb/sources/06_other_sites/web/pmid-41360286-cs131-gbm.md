# Cs-131 Collagen Tile (GammaTile) for Recurrent Glioblastoma

## Source
- PMID: 41360286
- Practical Radiation Oncology 2026 Mar-Apr;16(2):e101-e107
- MD Anderson Cancer Center, Houston

## Methods
- 15 adults with recurrent IDH-wildtype GBM
- All previously irradiated (60 Gy/30 fx most common)
- Re-resection + intraoperative Cs-131 collagen tile implantation
- Median 6.5 tiles per patient (range 3-13)
- Prescription: 60 Gy at 5 mm depth
- Median 15 months between prior EBRT and Cs-131 (range 8.9-47)

## Outcomes
- Median follow-up: 13 months
- Median overall survival: not reached (95% CI 6.7-NR)
- Median time to progression: 9 months
- Cumulative incidence of first progression: 53.3%
- Failure patterns: local (n=2), marginal (n=2), distant (n=3), combined (n=1)
- 1 grade 3 radionecrosis (resolved with bevacizumab)
- No grade 4-5 toxicities

## Key takeaways
- Cs-131 (half-life 9.7 days) is now the dominant intraoperative BT agent for brain
- Replaces I-125 GliaSite (which was withdrawn from market in 2017)
- FDA-approved as GammaTile (GT Medical Technologies)
- 60 Gy at 5 mm is the standard prescription depth/dose
- Rapid dose fall-off limits normal brain dose
- Indicated for: recurrent glioma, newly diagnosed at re-resection, brain metastases
