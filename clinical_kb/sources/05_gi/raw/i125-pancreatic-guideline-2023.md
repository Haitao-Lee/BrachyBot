# Guidelines for permanent iodine-125 seed interstitial brachytherapy for pancreatic cancer (2023 edition)

**Source:** Journal of Cancer Research and Therapeutics, 2024;20:1124-9
**DOI:** 10.4103/jcrt.jcrt_2368_23
**Organization:** Chinese College of Interventionalists, Chinese Medical Doctor Association

---

## Abstract

The incidence of pancreatic cancer is increasing worldwide. Approximately 60% of patients with pancreatic cancer have distant metastases at the time of diagnosis. Permanent iodine-125 seed interstitial brachytherapy has been used as a minimally invasive technique in the treatment of pancreatic cancer.

In 2023, the Chinese College of Interventionalists organized a group of multidisciplinary experts to compose guidelines for this treatment modality.

## Key Recommendations

### 1. Concept and Principles
- I-125 seeds: 4.5 mm × 0.8 mm, nickel titanium alloy envelope
- Half-life: 59.6 days
- Average energy: 35.5 KeV (gamma) + 27.4/31.4 KeV (X-rays)
- Mechanism: Inhibit cell proliferation, induce apoptosis, kill tumor cells

### 2. Implanting Methods
- **CT-guided percutaneous**: Most commonly used, high resolution
- **EUS-guided**: For small lesions around GI tract
- **Biliary stent with I-125**: For pancreatic head cancer with obstructive jaundice
- **Surgical implantation**: When tumor cannot be radically removed

### 3. Indications
- Tumors invading important structures, cannot be resected
- Patients cannot tolerate surgery
- Patients refuse surgical resection
- Disease progression after systemic therapy
- Residual lesions during surgery

### 4. Contraindications
**Absolute:**
- Cachexia and severe organ dysfunction
- Severe coagulation dysfunction (platelet <50×10⁹/L)
- Expected life span <3 months
- ECOG score ≥3

**Relative:**
- Concurrent distant metastasis (can still do for symptom relief)
- Obstructive jaundice (drain first)
- Tumor diameter >7 cm

### 5. Treatment Planning
- CT scan: 0.5 mm slice thickness
- I-125 seed activity: 0.3-0.8 mCi
- PTV: GTV + 1 cm margin
- Prescribed dose: 110-130 Gy
- Use TPS for dose-volume histogram (DVH) evaluation

### 6. Procedural Operation
- Position: Supine (most common)
- Anesthesia: IV general anesthesia + moderate sedation
- Needle: 18-gauge puncture needle
- Fluorouracil 0.05 ml bolus per seed to prevent tumor seeding
- Post-procedure CT verification

### 7. Complications
- **Bleeding**: Manage with needle repositioning, blood transfusion if >500 ml
- **Pancreatitis**: Monitor amylase levels
- **Seed migration**: Rare with proper technique
