# Contact X-Ray Brachytherapy (Papillon Technique) for Rectal Cancer

- **URL**: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1538472116300102
- **URL (PubMed)**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27083256/
- **Title**: Endoluminal Contact X-Ray Brachytherapy (Papillon) for Early Rectal Cancer
- **Authors**: Gerard JP, et al.
- **Journal**: Radiotherapy and Oncology / Clinical Oncology
- **Year**: 2016 (technique originated 1973, modern series 2008-2015)
- **Source type**: Major clinical series + technique description

## Background
- Papillon technique developed by Prof. Jean Papillon in Lyon, France (1973)
- Originally combined with external beam radiotherapy (EBRT) for early T1/T2 rectal cancer
- Modern renaissance: OPERA trial (2023) — neoadjuvant Papillon vs EBRT boost

## Indications
- Early T1N0 rectal adenocarcinoma (mobile, exophytic, well-differentiated)
- T2N0 < 5 cm diameter (selective)
- Patients medically unfit for or refusing surgery (TME)
- Local recurrence after surgery (selected)
- Pre-operative downstaging boost (OPERA trial)

## Equipment
- 50 kVp X-ray tube (Philips RT 50 / Gulmay / Papillon 50)
- Endorectal applicator: 3 cm diameter proctoscope
- SSD (source-skin distance) 4-5 cm
- HVL ~ 1.5 mm Al
- Dose rate: ~ 20 Gy/min at contact surface
- Quick, outpatient procedure

## Dose/Fractionation
- **50 kV surface dose prescription (classic)**
- 30 Gy in 3 fractions over 4 weeks (one fraction every 1-2 weeks)
- Or 50-70 Gy in 5-7 fractions over 6-8 weeks (combined with EBRT)
- Combined EBRT 39 Gy in 13 fractions + 3 x 10 Gy contact boost
- OPERA trial: 90 Gy in 3 fractions contact vs EBRT boost

## Technique
- Bowel preparation (enema)
- Lithotomy position
- Rigid proctoscope introduced
- Tumor visualized directly
- 3-5 second exposure for 10-30 Gy per fraction
- 2-3 mm fall-off per mm depth (very steep)
- Treats mucosa/submucosa only; spares deeper tissues

## Clinical Outcomes
- T1N0 local control: 85-95% (organ preservation)
- 5-year overall survival: 70-85% (T1, organ-preserved)
- OPERA trial: 79% organ preservation at 3 years
- Salvage surgery still possible for local recurrence

## Toxicity
- Acute: proctitis (10-20% grade 1-2)
- Late: rectal bleeding (5-10%), stenosis (< 5%), fistula (rare)
- Late radiation proctitis: usually self-limited

## Comparison with EBRT Boost
- Higher biological dose to surface
- Less toxicity to surrounding tissues
- Better organ preservation in OPERA trial
- Cost-effective, outpatient

## Key Findings
- Excellent for patient unfit for or refusing surgery
- 50 kV contact therapy = highest surface dose, rapid falloff
- Best for T1N0 mobile exophytic tumors
- Modern OPERA trial validated as organ-preservation option
- Limited to lesions within 12-15 cm of anal verge
