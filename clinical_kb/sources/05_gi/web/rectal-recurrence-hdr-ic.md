# HDR Intracavitary Brachytherapy for Recurrent Rectal Cancer

- **URL**: https://www.redjournal.org/article/S0360-3016(18)30086-1/fulltext
- **Title**: Brachytherapy in the management of locally recurrent rectal cancer
- **Authors**: Vuong T, et al.
- **Journal**: International Journal of Radiation Oncology Biology Physics (Red Journal)
- **Year**: 2018
- **Source type**: Clinical series + review

## Background
- Locally recurrent rectal cancer after prior EBRT is challenging
- Re-irradiation limited by normal tissue tolerance
- BT delivers high dose to limited volume
- Allows re-treatment with acceptable toxicity

## BT Indications
- Local recurrence after prior pelvic EBRT
- Small volume recurrence (< 5 cm)
- Axial/sacral/anastomotic recurrence
- Patients refusing exenteration
- Palliation of bleeding/pain

## BT Technique
- Transrectal ultrasound/CT-guided intracavitary applicator
- Endorectal balloon applicator
- Interstitial multi-catheter implant for larger lesions
- HDR Ir-192 afterloading
- 3D CT/MRI-guided planning

## Dose/Fractionation
- 5-6 Gy x 5-6 fractions (cumulative 25-36 Gy)
- Twice daily with 6-hour interval
- 4 Gy x 8 fractions over 4-5 days
- EQD2: 25-40 Gy (depends on α/β assumption)
- Re-irradiation context: limit total cumulative dose

## Clinical Outcomes
- Local control 1-year: 50-70%
- Median survival: 18-30 months
- Pain relief: 60-80%
- Bleeding control: 80-90%

## OAR Constraints (Re-irradiation)
- Cumulative rectal D2cc < 75-90 Gy EQD2
- Bladder cumulative D2cc < 80-90 Gy EQD2
- Small bowel: limit dose
- S2/S3 nerve roots: monitor dose

## Key Findings
- HDR IC BT is option for select recurrent rectal cancer
- Avoids surgical morbidity of exenteration
- Combined with EBRT for definitive treatment
- Brachytherapy alone for palliation
- 3D image guidance essential for re-irradiation
