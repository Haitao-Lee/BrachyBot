# I-125 Seed Implantation for Pancreatic Cancer: Clinical Evidence

- **URL**: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5684395/
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28503264/
- **Title**: Permanent iodine-125 brachytherapy for patients with locally advanced pancreatic cancer
- **Authors**: Wang J, Jiang Y, Li J, et al.
- **Journal**: Medicine (Baltimore) / Oncotarget
- **Year**: 2017
- **Source type**: Clinical series + meta-analysis

## Study Population
- 137 patients with locally advanced pancreatic cancer
- Laparotomy/percutaneous CT-guided I-125 seed implantation
- D90 range 110-160 Gy

## Clinical Outcomes
- Pain relief: 78% (within 2 weeks)
- Median OS: 10.6 months
- 1-year OS: 36%
- 2-year OS: 12%
- Local control 6 months: 71%

## Combination Therapy
- I-125 + EBRT: median OS 14-18 months
- I-125 + chemotherapy: improved survival vs I-125 alone
- I-125 + EBRT + chemo: best outcomes in retrospective series
- Common chemo: gemcitabine-based

## Dosimetric Outcomes
- D90 > 110 Gy associated with better local control
- V100 > 90% achievable in most cases
- Volume effect: smaller tumors better local control
- Post-implant CT at 24-48 hours standard

## Meta-Analysis Findings
- Sun et al. (2016): 12 studies, 1100+ patients
- I-125 + chemo vs chemo alone: 1-year OS 42% vs 22% (HR 0.65)
- I-125 significantly improves pain control
- Complications: GI bleeding 5-8%, pancreatitis 6-10%

## Volume Effect
- Tumor volume < 30 cm³: best outcomes
- Larger volumes: more complications, lower D90
- Staged implantation: option for > 7 cm tumors

## Key Findings
- I-125 seed implantation is established palliative option in China
- Standard of care in Chinese centers for LAPC
- D90 prescription 110-140 Gy common
- Activity 0.5-0.7 mCi per seed
- 0.5-1.0 cm spacing
- Combine with gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel for OS benefit
