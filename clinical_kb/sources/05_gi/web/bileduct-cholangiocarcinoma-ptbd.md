# Bile Duct / Cholangiocarcinoma Intraluminal HDR Brachytherapy

- **URL**: https://www.redjournal.org/article/S0360-3016(15)01055-8/fulltext
- **URL (PMC)**: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5644482/
- **Title**: Role of Brachytherapy in the Management of Cholangiocarcinoma
- **Authors**: Brunner TB, Seufferlein T, Wenz F, et al.
- **Year**: 2015
- **Source type**: Review + clinical practice recommendations

## Indications
- Unresectable extrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (Klatskin tumor)
- Locally advanced distal cholangiocarcinoma
- Recurrent cholangiocarcinoma after surgery
- Post PTBD/ERCP biliary drainage (typically after 1-2 weeks of drainage)
- Combined with EBRT for definitive treatment
- Adjuvant after surgical resection (high-risk R1)

## Implant Technique
- PTBD (percutaneous transhepatic biliary drainage) catheter tracts
- ERCP-guided nasobiliary catheter
- 6-10 Fr intrabiliary HDR catheter placed through existing drain tract
- Afterloading Ir-192 HDR source
- Single channel: 1-2 cm source spacing
- Multi-channel: improved target coverage
- Treatment length: 2-3 cm beyond tumor margins

## Dose/Fractionation
- HDR brachytherapy alone: 14-25 Gy in 3-5 fractions (5-7 Gy/fraction)
- Common: 5 Gy x 4-5 fractions
- Combined with EBRT: EBRT 45-50 Gy + HDR boost 10-20 Gy
- BID fractionation: 5 Gy x 2 separated by 6 hours
- BED ~ 30-50 Gy for BT alone component
- EQD2 conversion for α/β = 10: significant contribution to total dose

## Clinical Outcomes
- Median survival: 10-24 months (combined modality)
- 1-year local control: 50-80%
- 2-year survival: 20-40%
- Stent patency improved with BT (compared to stent alone)
- Bile duct patency: 70-90% at 6 months

## OAR Constraints
- Duodenum D2cc < 50-60 Gy EQD2
- Stomach D2cc < 50-60 Gy EQD2
- Spinal cord Dmax < 45 Gy EQD2
- Liver mean dose: minimize
- Common bile duct: high dose, but target

## Toxicity
- Cholangitis (10-20%)
- Bile duct stenosis (late, 5-10%)
- Hemobilia (< 5%)
- Duodenal ulcer (5-10%)
- PTBD-related complications

## Key Findings
- BT improves stent patency vs stent alone
- Combined EBRT + BT shows survival benefit in retrospective series
- Dose escalation with BT boost associated with better local control
- PROLONG trial evidence supports BT use
- Catheter positioning critical: confirmed by CT/fluoroscopy
