# Gastric Brachytherapy

- **URL**: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3664934/
- **URL**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23131250/
- **Title**: Brachytherapy for gastric cancer: review of clinical applications
- **Journal**: Radiation Oncology / Brachytherapy
- **Year**: 2012
- **Source type**: Clinical review

## Background
- Gastric brachytherapy is rare in modern practice
- Most gastric cancers treated with surgery + perioperative chemo
- EBRT (IMRT/VMAT) standard for unresectable/recurrent disease
- Intraluminal HDR brachytherapy has limited indications

## BT Indications
- Local recurrence at gastroesophageal anastomosis
- Cardia/gastric stump lesions
- Stomal recurrence after gastrectomy
- Palliation of bleeding tumor
- In combination with EBRT for unresectable cases

## BT Technique
- Endoscopic placement of intraluminal applicator
- Single-channel or 3-channel applicator
- HDR Ir-192 afterloading
- Prescription to 1 cm depth
- 6-8 mm applicator diameter

## Dose/Fractionation
- EBRT 45-50 Gy + HDR boost 10-15 Gy in 2-3 fractions
- Standalone palliative: 6 Gy x 3-4 fractions
- BED high but limited target coverage
- Image-guided CT-based planning

## Clinical Outcomes
- Limited case series
- 1-year local control: 50-70% (combined modality)
- Bleeding control: 70-80%
- Dysphagia relief: 60-80%

## OAR Constraints
- Mucosal surface: avoid > 150% prescription
- Adjacent stomach wall: < 60 Gy EQD2
- Liver: V30 < 30%
- Spinal cord: < 45 Gy EQD2
- Heart: V25 < 10%

## Key Findings
- Gastric BT is uncommon but option in select scenarios
- EBRT is dominant for gastric cancer
- BT has role for recurrence and palliation
- High-dose region limited to 1-2 cm from applicator
- Coverage of full thickness gastric wall challenging
