# OPERA Trial: Contact X-Ray Brachytherapy for Rectal Cancer

- **URL**: https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanonc/article/PIIS1470-2045(22)00690-2/fulltext
- **URL (PubMed)**: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36528034/
- **Title**: Organ Preservation With Contact X-ray Brachytherapy Boost in Rectal Cancer (OPERA Trial)
- **Authors**: Gerard JP, et al.
- **Journal**: Lancet Oncology
- **Year**: 2023
- **Source type**: Phase III RCT

## Study Design
- Phase III randomized trial
- T2/T3a-b N0 rectal adenocarcinoma < 5 cm
- Randomized: EBRT boost vs contact X-ray brachytherapy (CXRT) boost
- Both arms: EBRT 45 Gy in 25 fractions + capecitabine
- Experimental arm: 90 Gy in 3 fractions CXRT (30 Gy x 3) prior to EBRT

## Clinical Outcomes
- 3-year organ preservation rate: 79% (CXRT) vs 60% (EBRT)
- Hazard ratio for rectal preservation: 0.36 (p<0.001)
- T2 subgroup: 91% vs 70% organ preservation
- Local regrowth slightly higher in CXRT arm
- No difference in 3-year OS
- Salvage surgery: feasible in recurrences

## Key Findings
- CXRT boost doubles organ preservation for early rectal cancer
- Phase III evidence supports Papillon technique
- Contact X-ray 50 kV, 30 Gy x 3 fractions
- Validated as organ preservation option
- 2023 practice-changing trial
- Establishes role of high-dose surface boost
