# I-125 + Gemcitabine for Locally Advanced Pancreatic Cancer

- **URL**: https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1411279
- **URL**: https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanonc/article/PIIS1470-2045(15)00440-3/fulltext
- **Title**: LAPC trial: I-125 seed + chemotherapy vs chemotherapy alone
- **Year**: 2015-2018
- **Source type**: Clinical trial evidence

## Background
- Standard of care for LAPC: gemcitabine-based chemo
- Local progression common with chemo alone
- I-125 seeds provide local control
- Chinese centers pioneered combined therapy

## Clinical Evidence

### Study 1: 137 patient cohort
- I-125 + gemcitabine: median OS 12.4 months
- Gemcitabine alone: median OS 7.2 months
- 1-year OS: 50% vs 25%
- Pain control: 85% vs 35%

### Study 2: 280 patient multi-center
- I-125 + SBRT + chemo: median OS 16.5 months
- Chemo alone: 10.8 months
- Local progression 1-year: 25% vs 60%

## Treatment Protocol
- Pre-op planning CT
- 18-G needle insertion under CT guidance
- I-125 seed activity 0.5-0.6 mCi
- Spacing 0.5-1.0 cm
- D90 110-140 Gy
- Post-implant verification CT
- Gemcitabine 1000 mg/m² weekly

## Toxicity
- GI bleeding: 5-8% (grade 3-4)
- Pancreatitis: 6-10%
- Pancreatic fistula: < 5%
- Seed migration: < 1%
- Procedural: pneumothorax, vascular injury (< 1%)

## Key Findings
- I-125 + chemo improves OS vs chemo alone
- Local control major benefit
- Acceptable toxicity profile
- Most evidence from Chinese centers
- Western adoption limited but growing
- Cost-effective option in resource-limited settings
