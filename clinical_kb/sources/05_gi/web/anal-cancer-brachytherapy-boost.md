# Anal Cancer Brachytherapy Boost

- **URL**: https://www.redjournal.org/article/S0360-3016(18)30132-5/fulltext
- **URL**: https://www.astro.org/Daily-Practice/ARS-ROPA/Clinical-Practice
- **Title**: Brachytherapy boost for anal cancer: GEC-ESTRO/ABS/ESTRO consensus
- **Authors**: Sun Myint A, et al.
- **Journal**: Radiotherapy and Oncology / Brachytherapy
- **Year**: 2018
- **Source type**: Multisociety consensus

## Background
- Anal cancer is primarily treated with definitive chemoradiation
- Nigro protocol: 5-FU + mitomycin C + RT
- Standard EBRT 50.4-59.4 Gy to primary tumor
- BT boost controversial but option in select cases

## BT Indications
- Boost for residual disease at 4-6 weeks post-EBRT
- Salvage for local recurrence
- Small T1-T2N0 lesions as sole boost technique
- Patients with poor response to EBRT

## BT Technique
- Intracavitary mold technique (preferred for anal canal)
- Interstitial multi-catheter implant (for tumor extension)
- HDR Ir-192 afterloading
- Custom applicator shaped to anal canal anatomy
- 5-7 channels typically

## Dose/Fractionation
- EBRT 45-50.4 Gy + HDR boost 10-20 Gy in 2-5 fractions
- Single dose HDR 5-6 Gy x 2-3 fractions
- Prescription to clinical target volume
- CTV = tumor bed + 1 cm margin
- 3D image-guided planning (CT or MRI)

## Clinical Outcomes
- Local control 90%+ when combined with chemo
- Comparable to EBRT boost
- Spares perineal skin vs EBRT boost
- Reduced treatment time vs EBRT boost

## OAR Constraints
- Perineal skin: V90 < 10 cc < 30 Gy EQD2
- Vaginal mucosa: max < 50 Gy EQD2
- Urethra: max < 50 Gy EQD2
- Rectum: D2cc < 50-60 Gy EQD2
- Bladder: D2cc < 60-65 Gy EQD2

## Key Findings
- BT boost is an option but rarely used in modern practice
- Standard EBRT 50.4-59.4 Gy with chemo is dominant
- BT reserved for very small lesions or boost scenarios
- Interstitial technique: higher dose to tumor, lower to skin
- 3D planning mandatory for OAR sparing
