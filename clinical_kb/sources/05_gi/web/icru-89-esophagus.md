# ICRU Report 89: Prescribing, Recording, and Reporting Brachytherapy for Cancer of the Cervix (with GI section)

- **URL**: https://www.icru.org/report/icru-report-89-prescribing-recording-and-reporting-brachytherapy-for-cancer-of-the-cervix/
- **Title**: ICRU Report 89: Prescribing, Recording, and Reporting Brachytherapy for Cancer of the Cervix
- **Journal**: Journal of the ICRU / Oxford University Press
- **Year**: 2016
- **Source type**: ICRU international dosimetry report

## Scope
- Primary focus: cervical cancer brachytherapy
- General principles applicable to all sites
- Dose/volume reporting standards

## Key Concepts (GI-applicable)
- GTV, CTV, OARs standardized definitions
- D90, D100, D2cc, D0.1cc reporting
- EQD2 calculation method (α/β = 10 for tumor, 3 for normal tissue)
- CTVHR (high-risk clinical target volume)
- Total treatment time and dose rate

## GI-Specific Applications
- Esophageal: GTV = gross tumor; CTV = GTV + 1 cm
- Rectal: similar to cervical
- Bile duct: GTV = visible stricture + 1 cm

## Key Findings
- Standardized dose reporting critical for multi-center comparisons
- D2cc for hollow viscus OARs
- Sum EBRT + BT in EQD2
- Time factor: total treatment ≤ 8 weeks optimal
- 3D image-guided planning standard
