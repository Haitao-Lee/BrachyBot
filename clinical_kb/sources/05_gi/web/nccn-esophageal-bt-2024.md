# NCCN Guidelines for Esophageal Cancer Brachytherapy

- **URL**: https://www.nccn.org/guidelines
- **Title**: NCCN Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology: Esophageal and GE Junction Cancers
- **Year**: 2024 (Version 4.2024)
- **Source type**: NCCN clinical practice guideline

## Indications
- Definitive treatment of medically inoperable esophageal cancer
- Palliation of dysphagia
- Brachytherapy boost after EBRT in select cases
- Locally advanced unresectable disease

## Recommended Dose
- EBRT 50-50.4 Gy (1.8-2 Gy/fraction) with concurrent chemo
- HDR brachytherapy boost: 10-20 Gy in 2-5 fractions
- Prescription to 1 cm depth
- 6-8 weeks total treatment time

## Palliative BT
- 6-8 Gy x 2-3 fractions for dysphagia
- 10-12 Gy single fraction
- Brachytherapy monotherapy option

## Endorsement of ABS
- References ABS 2014 guidelines
- Image-guided planning recommended
- 3D CT-based planning preferred
- Maximum surface dose < 150% prescription

## Key Findings
- NCCN acknowledges brachytherapy as option
- Limited data; major role in palliation
- EBRT dominant in US practice
- BT boost option for unresectable disease
- 1 cm depth prescription standard
