# ABS 2014 Esophageal Brachytherapy Guidelines

- **URL**: https://www.americanbrachytherapy.org/ABS/document-server/index.cfm?documentID=6686
- **Title**: American Brachytherapy Society (ABS) consensus guidelines for brachytherapy of esophageal cancer
- **Authors**: Shah AK, Levine MS, Morgan J, et al. (ABS Brachytherapy esophageal cancer working group)
- **Journal**: Brachytherapy
- **Year**: 2014 (also referenced in IJROBP context)
- **Source type**: Society consensus guideline

## Key Recommendations

### Indications
- Definitive radiotherapy boost for medically inoperable or unresectable esophageal cancer
- Palliation of dysphagia in advanced/metastatic disease
- Boost after external beam radiotherapy (50 Gy EBRT + 10-20 Gy HDR boost)
- Standalone palliation in patients unsuitable for EBRT

### Dose/Fractionation (Curative Boost)
- EBRT 45-50 Gy in 25 fractions + HDR boost 10-20 Gy in 2-5 fractions
- Boost prescription to 1 cm depth from source axis
- Common fractionation: 5 Gy x 2-4 fractions (one to two weeks)
- Alternative: 6-8 Gy x 2 fractions weekly
- BID fractionation with minimum 6-hour inter-fraction interval

### Palliative
- Single fraction 8-12 Gy or 6 Gy x 3 fractions
- 10 Gy in 2 fractions
- Prescription to 1 cm depth

### Technique
- HDR Ir-192 afterloading
- Applicator diameter 8-10 mm (typically 10 mm)
- Single or multi-channel endoluminal applicator
- Source position 2-3 cm proximal and distal to tumor margin
- CT-based 3D planning preferred
- GTV = visible tumor + 2-3 cm longitudinal margin
- CTV = GTV + 1 cm radial margin (full thickness esophageal wall)

### Dose Limits / OAR Constraints
- Maximum mucosal point dose: < 150% of prescription
- Spinal cord cumulative (EBRT + BT): < 45-50 Gy EQD2
- Lung V20 (EBRT constraints)
- Heart V25 < 10% (EBRT constraints)

### Follow-Up
- Endoscopy + biopsy at 3 months
- CT/PET at 6 months
- Dilatation for stricture

### Key Findings
- Brachytherapy provides excellent dysphagia palliation (70-90% relief)
- 1-year local control ~30-50% in definitive setting
- Fistula risk < 5-10% with proper technique
- Survival benefit of BT boost remains debated

## Comparison
- Comparable to EBRT boost for local control
- Lower cost and shorter treatment course
- Higher risk of mucosal ulceration/fistula if overdosed
