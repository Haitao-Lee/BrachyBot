## Document Type
ESMO clinical practice guidelines with brachytherapy sections (medical oncology perspective, with radiation oncology input).

## Cervical Cancer (Marth et al., 2017, 2023 update)
**"Cervical cancer: ESMO Clinical Practice Guidelines for diagnosis, treatment and follow-up."** *Annals of Oncology* 2017;28(suppl 4):iv72-iv83. Updated 2023.

- Concomitant chemoradiotherapy + brachytherapy is the **standard treatment** for locally advanced disease
- IGABT (per GEC-ESTRO/ICRU 89) is the recommended BT approach
- HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2 (aim 90 Gy)
- Cisplatin 40 mg/m² weekly during EBRT

## Endometrial Cancer (Colombo et al., 2013; 2021 update)
- Adjuvant vaginal cuff brachytherapy (VCB) for intermediate-risk disease
- VCB dose: 21–24 Gy in 3–4 fx to 5 mm depth, or equivalent (HDR)
- Avoids need for pelvic EBRT in selected intermediate-risk

## Prostate Cancer (Parker et al., 2020; 2023)
- For low-risk: Active surveillance preferred; EBRT or LDR/HDR brachytherapy monotherapy if intervention needed
- For intermediate-risk: EBRT ± ADT, or radical prostatectomy, or LDR/HDR BT
- For high-risk: EBRT + long-term ADT; BT boost optional at experienced centers

## Head and Neck Cancer (Machiels et al., 2020)
- BT (interstitial LDR/HDR) considered for small T1-T2 NO lesions of lip, oral cavity, oropharynx

## Skin (2022)
- Locally advanced BCC and SCC: surgery preferred; BT (superficial or interstitial) for inoperable cases
