## Document Type
ARS Appropriate Use Criteria (AUC) documents — formal multidisciplinary consensus based on the RAND/UCLA methodology, addressing disease-specific indications and techniques.

## Key Brachytherapy AUC Documents

### Cervical Cancer (2012, 2014, 2020)
- IGABT is the standard of care
- HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2 (α/β=10)
- Combined intracavitary + interstitial (IC/IS) recommended for HR-CTV > 4 cm

### Endometrial Cancer (Vaginal Cylinder BT) (2014, 2018)
- Vaginal cuff brachytherapy for high-intermediate-risk disease
- Adjuvant VCB after surgery for stage I-II disease
- Dose: 7 Gy × 3 (to 5 mm depth) or equivalent
- Cylinder diameter selection: largest that fits comfortably (typically 3.0–3.5 cm)

### Prostate Cancer (2013, 2017, 2022)
- LDR monotherapy for low-risk
- LDR or HDR boost with EBRT for intermediate/high-risk
- HDR monotherapy as an alternative for low/favorable intermediate-risk

### Head and Neck Cancer (2016, 2018)
- BT alone (interstitial) for small T1-T2 NO lesions
- BT boost (10–20 Gy) after EBRT for larger lesions
- Most common site: lip, oral cavity, oropharynx, nasal vestibule

### Breast (APBI) (2016, 2022)
- Multi-catheter interstitial BT has longest evidence base
- ASTRO/ABS/ASCO consensus categorizes patients as "Suitable", "Cautionary", "Unsuitable"

### Sarcoma (2015)
- BT as boost (LDR 15–20 Gy over 2–4 days) or monotherapy for small-volume disease
- Catheter-based, often intraoperative placement

### Skin (2018, 2022)
- Superficial (Leipzig/HAM applicators) for lesions ≤ 5 mm depth
- Custom mold or flap for irregular surface
- Dose: 40–50 Gy in 8–10 fx, or 36 Gy in 6 fx, depending on depth
