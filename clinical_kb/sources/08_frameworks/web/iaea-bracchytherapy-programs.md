## Document Type
International radiation oncology capacity and infrastructure reports.

## DIRAC (Directory of RAdiotherapy Centres)
- Database tracking all radiotherapy facilities worldwide
- 2014 data: ~7000 EBRT machines serving 60% of global need
- BT access is markedly uneven — high-income countries have ~10× more BT machines per cancer case
- Brachytherapy afterloaders are present in ~70% of all radiotherapy centers globally (variable case load)

## IAEA Recommendations for BT Programs

### Infrastructure (per IAEA Human Health Series)
- HDR afterloader (³⁰Co or ¹⁹²Ir) is the standard
- PDR afterloaders accepted in some countries
- LDR (manual or remote afterloading) is being phased out
- Required: TPS (treatment planning system) with image import, applicator library, dedicated shielded room

### Training
- IAEA supports brachytherapy schools (e.g., Vienna, ICTP Trieste, ESTRO courses)
- Curriculum: 1–2 weeks didactic + hands-on
- Required: radiation oncologist, medical physicist, dosimetrist, RTT training
- Maintenance: ongoing CME; visiting expert programmes

### Quality Assurance
- Source calibration: well-type chamber traceable to national standards lab
- Annual end-to-end dosimetry audit (IAEA/WHO postal TLD programme)
- Patient-specific QA: independent plan check, in-vivo dosimetry for selected cases

## IAEA Documents on Brachytherapy (selection)

- **IAEA Human Health Series No. 30**: "The Transition from 2-D Brachytherapy to 3-D High Dose Rate Brachytherapy" (2015)
- **IAEA TRS-430**: "Commissioning and Quality Assurance of Computerized Planning Systems for External Beam and Brachytherapy" (2004)
- **IAEA TRS-492**: "Calibration of Brachytherapy Sources" (in development)

## Global Brachytherapy Disparities (key figures)
- High-income countries: ~95% access to BT
- Low-income countries: < 5% of eligible cervical cancer patients receive BT
- GLOBOCAN 2020: 604,000 new cervical cancer cases worldwide, 342,000 deaths — most in LMIC
