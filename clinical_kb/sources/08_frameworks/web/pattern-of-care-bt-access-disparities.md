## Patterns of Care in Brachytherapy — Cervical Cancer

### US (NCDB/SEER analysis 2010–2018)
- ~ 60–65% of Stage IIB–IVA cervical cancer patients receive BT (in compliant centers)
- BT omission is associated with worse OS (HR 1.5–1.7)
- African-American and uninsured patients have lower BT utilization
- Centers with high BT volume have better outcomes

### Europe (ESGO/ESTRO/ESP audits)
- Wide variation between countries (Northern Europe > Eastern Europe)
- IGABT adoption (per GEC-ESTRO/ICRU 89): ~50% of centers in 2018, growing

### Global (GLOBOCAN 2020)
- Cervical cancer incidence: 604,000 new cases, 342,000 deaths
- > 80% of cases in low- and middle-income countries (LMIC)
- In LMIC, BT access is < 30% of patients who need it
- Reasons: limited infrastructure, no training, no sources, no afterloaders

## Prostate Brachytherapy (US NCDB analysis)
- Steady decline in LDR use since 2000s
- ~ 3–4% of newly diagnosed low-risk patients receive LDR
- HDR brachytherapy monotherapy growing at academic centers

## Breast APBI
- ASTRO consensus broadened suitable category in 2016
- Adoption has grown; ~ 10–15% of suitable patients in US receive APBI
- Multi-catheter interstitial BT has strongest evidence

## Head and Neck
- BT use for early-stage disease has declined with advent of IMRT and SBRT
- Still favored for T1-T2 lesions of lip and selected oropharynx

## Global Access Disparities

| Region | HDR BT per million population | Capacity vs need |
|--------|------------------------------|------------------|

| Sub-Saharan Africa | < 0.5 | < 10% of need |
| South Asia | ~ 0.5 | < 20% of need |
