# American Brachytherapy Society (ABS) Consensus Statements for Breast Brachytherapy (APBI + Boost)

**Source URL:** https://www.americanbrachytherapy.org/guidelines
**Society:** American Brachytherapy Society
**Years:** 2009, 2012, 2016, 2023
**Journal:** Brachytherapy
**Fetched:** 2026-06-17

## Document Type
ABS consensus guidelines for accelerated partial breast irradiation (APBI) and brachytherapy boost after whole-breast irradiation.

## Key Statements

### 2009 APBI Statement (Shah et al.)
**"The American Brachytherapy Society consensus statement for accelerated partial breast irradiation"** — first formal ABS APBI guidelines.

### 2012 APBI Update (Shah et al.)
**"The American Brachytherapy Society consensus statement on accelerated partial breast irradiation"** *Brachytherapy* 2012 — refined patient selection criteria.

### 2016 Consensus (Cuttino et al.) - ASTRO/ASCO/ABS Joint
Cuttino LW, Arthur DW, Vicini F, Todor D, Julian TB, Mukhopadhyay N. **"The American Society for Radiation Oncology (ASTRO) and the American Brachytherapy Society (ABS) consensus statement on multichannel applicator-based brachytherapy for accelerated partial breast irradiation"** *Brachytherapy* 2016;15(5):544-551.

## ABS Patient Selection for APBI (2012)

| Suitable | Cautionary | Unsuitable |
|----------|------------|------------|
| Age ≥ 60, tumor ≤ 2 cm, ER+, node-neg, margins ≥ 2 mm, no LVSI | Age 50–59, tumor 2–3 cm, margin < 2 mm | Age < 50, tumor > 3 cm, multifocal, EIC, LVI+ |

## Common APBI Fractionation Schemes (Multi-catheter or Balloon-based)

| Modality | Dose | Fractions | Schedule |
|----------|------|-----------|----------|
| Multi-catheter (HDR) | 34 Gy | 10 | BID × 5 days |
| Multi-catheter (HDR) | 32 Gy | 8 | BID × 4 days |
| Multi-catheter (HDR) | 30 Gy | 6 | QD × 6 days (5 fx/wk) |
| Multi-catheter (HDR) | 28 Gy | 7 | BID × 3.5 days |
| Balloon (SAVI, MammoSite) | 34 Gy | 10 | BID × 5 days |

## Boost Brachytherapy (after 50 Gy WBI)

| Modality | Dose | Fractions |
|----------|------|-----------|
| HDR interstitial | 10–15 Gy | 4–8 fx |
| LDR interstitial | 20–25 Gy | 1 implant (iridium wires, dose rate 0.5 Gy/h) |
| Single-catheter balloon | 10–15 Gy | 2–3 fx |
