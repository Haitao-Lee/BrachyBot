## Key Brachytherapy-Related Documents

### APBI Consensus (2009, 2016, 2022 updates)
Smith BD, Arthur DW, Buchholz TA, Haffty BG, Hahn CA, Hardenbergh PH, Julian TB, Marks LB, Todor DA, Vicini FA, Whelan TJ, White J, Wo JY, Harris JR. **"Accelerated partial breast irradiation consensus statement from the American Society for Radiation Oncology (ASTRO)."** *Int J Radiat Oncol Biol Phys* 2009;74(4):987-1001.

- Defines "Suitable", "Cautionary", "Unsuitable" patient categories
- 2016 update (Correa et al., *IJROBP* 2016) — expanded candidates, included electronic brachytherapy

### Consensus Guideline on Postoperative Radiation Therapy (Prostate)
- ABS/ASTRO joint guideline for EBRT + BT boost post-prostatectomy
- For adverse pathology: consider LDR (110 Gy I-125) or HDR (15 Gy single fx) boost

### Clinical Practice Statement on High-Dose-Rate Brachytherapy for Prostate Cancer (2022 update)
ASTRO recognizes HDR as standard-of-care option for low/intermediate-risk and as boost for higher risk.

### Cervical Cancer Brachytherapy
- ASTRO endorses NCCN cervical cancer guidelines
- Reaffirms IGABT (per GEC-ESTRO/ABS) as standard

### Oligometastatic Disease
**"Radiation Therapy for Oligometastatic Cancer: An ASTRO/ESTRO Clinical Practice Guideline"** (Lievens et al., 2023) — addresses use of SBRT and conventional RT, including BT for select patients.

### Sarcoma Brachytherapy
ASTRO supports the role of brachytherapy in soft tissue sarcoma (typically surface or interstitial LDR/HDR, used as boost or monotherapy for select cases).
