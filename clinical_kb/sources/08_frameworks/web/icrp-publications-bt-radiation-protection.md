# ICRP Publications Relevant to Brachytherapy Radiation Protection

**Source URL:** https://www.icrp.org
**Organization:** International Commission on Radiological Protection (ICRP)
**Years:** Multiple; key recent reports
**Fetched:** 2026-06-17

---

## Document Type
ICRP international recommendations on radiation protection for patients, staff, and the public in brachytherapy practice.

## Key Publications

### ICRP Publication 97: Prevention of High-Dose-Rate Brachytherapy Accidents (2005)
- Lessons learned from 35 HDR BT accidents worldwide (1960s–2000s)
- Common causes: source retraction failure, communication errors, plan/dose delivery mismatches
- Recommendations: independent second check, time-out, emergency source retraction drills, continuous monitoring

### ICRP Publication 98: Radiation Safety Aspects of Brachytherapy for Prostate Cancer Using Permanently Implanted Sources (2005)
- Covers ¹²⁵I, ¹⁰³Pd, ¹³¹Cs permanent implants
- Patient release criteria (e.g., dose to family members < 1 mSv/yr)
- Caregiver instructions
- Cremation, surgery, autopsy considerations

### ICRP Publication 112: Preventing Accidental Exposures from New External Beam Radiation Therapy Technologies (2009)
- Includes high-dose-rate BT in its safety framework
- Risk-informed safety culture recommendations

### ICRP Publication 113: Education and Training in Radiological Protection for Diagnostic and Interventional Procedures (2009)
- Defines competencies for brachytherapy staff
- Curriculum for radiation oncologists, physicists, RTTs

### ICRP Publication 127: Radiological Protection in Ion Beam Radiotherapy (2014)
- Mostly about proton/heavy-ion therapy, but principles extend to BT

### ICRP Publication 139: Occupational Radiological Protection in Brachytherapy (2018)
- Specific guidance on staff protection
- Dose limits: 20 mSv/yr (whole body), 500 mSv/yr (skin, hands, feet)
- HDR afterloader use: minimize exposure via remote loading
- Permanent implant: simple precautions (distance, time, shielding)
- Pregnant staff: declaration, dose monitoring, role adjustment

## Key Safety Recommendations (Cross-cutting)
- HDR afterloaders in shielded rooms
- Two-person check for every treatment
- Independent verification of source strength
- Quality audits (TG-43 + MBDCA dual reporting)
- Source inventory and tracking
- Patient discharge criteria based on dose rate at 1 m
