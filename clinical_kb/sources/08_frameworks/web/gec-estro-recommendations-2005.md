## Document Type
GEC-ESTRO (Groupe Européen de Curiethérapie — European Society for Radiotherapy & Oncology) consensus guideline — Part I of a multipart series.

## Key Concept
Established the **3D image-guided adaptive brachytherapy (IGABT)** paradigm for cervix cancer, replacing the legacy Manchester/Fletcher point-A dosing. MRI is the modality of choice (CT is acceptable if MRI contraindicated).

## Defined Target Volumes

| Volume | Definition |
|--------|-----------|
| **GTV (BT)** | Gross tumor visible on imaging at time of brachytherapy |
| **HR-CTV (High-Risk CTV)** | GTV + entire cervix + parametrial tissue involved at diagnosis; presumed residual disease at BT |
| **IR-CTV (Intermediate-Risk CTV)** | HR-CTV + 5–10 mm margin for microscopic disease; reflects initial tumor extent |
| **OAR contouring** | Bladder, rectum, sigmoid, small bowel — outer wall, not the content |

## Dose Reporting Standard
- Cumulative **EQD2** (α/β = 10 Gy for tumor, α/β = 3 Gy for OAR)
- Reference dose points: HR-CTV D90, IR-CTV D90, OAR D2cc, D0.1cc, D1cc
- BT dose prescribed such that HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2 (α/β=10) when combined with 45–50 Gy EBRT

## Clinical Impact
This paper is the foundation of modern IGABT and underpins the EMBRACE studies.
