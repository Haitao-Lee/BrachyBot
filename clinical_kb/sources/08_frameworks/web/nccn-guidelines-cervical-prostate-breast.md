## NCCN Cervical Cancer Guideline (Cervical Cancer v.2024)

### Brachytherapy Indications
- Standard of care for **all locally advanced cervical cancers** (Stage IIB–IVA) and most Stage IB2–IIA2
- Used as boost after external beam radiotherapy (EBRT) ± concurrent cisplatin
- Image-guided adaptive brachytherapy (IGABT) is **the recommended approach**
- High-dose-rate (HDR) is preferred; LDR is acceptable

### NCCN-Recommended Dose (Combined EBRT + BT)
- EBRT: 45 Gy in 25 fx (± SIB to involved nodes)
- BT (HDR): 5–6 × 5–9 Gy (per implant, typically 4 fx in practice)
- Cumulative dose to HR-CTV D90: **≥ 85 Gy EQD2** (α/β=10)

### NCCN OAR Constraints
| Organ | D2cc (EQD2, α/β=3) |
|-------|----------------------|
| Bladder | ≤ 80–85 Gy |
| Rectum | ≤ 65–75 Gy |
| Sigmoid | ≤ 65–75 Gy |

## NCCN Prostate Cancer Guideline (Prostate Cancer v.2024)

### Brachytherapy Modalities
- **LDR monotherapy**: low-risk disease (Gleason ≤ 6, PSA < 10, cT1c–T2a)
- **LDR boost** (with EBRT): intermediate/high-risk
- **HDR boost** (with EBRT): preferred for unfavorable intermediate/high-risk per several academic centers
- **HDR monotherapy**: NCCN acknowledges emerging option for low- and favorable intermediate-risk

### LDR Monotherapy Doses
| Isotope | Dose |
|---------|------|
| I-125 | 144–145 Gy |
| Pd-103 | 125 Gy |
| Cs-131 | 115 Gy |

## NCCN Breast Cancer Guideline (Breast Cancer v.2024)

### APBI (Accelerated Partial Breast Irradiation) — NCCN-endorsed Options
- Multi-catheter interstitial HDR (strongest evidence)
- Single-entry intracavitary balloon or strut-based (SAVI, Contura, MammoSite)
- Permanent interstitial seed implant (LDR, 90–110 Gy)
- External beam APBI (3D-CRT, IMRT, protons) — included since 2009
- Intraoperative radiation therapy (IORT) — limited recommendation

### NCCN-Recommended APBI Dose Schemes
- 34 Gy in 10 fx BID (most common)
- 38.5 Gy in 10 fx BID
- 32 Gy in 8 fx BID

### Patient Selection
- Generally: age ≥ 50, tumor ≤ 2 cm, ER+, node-negative, margins ≥ 2 mm, no extensive intraductal component
