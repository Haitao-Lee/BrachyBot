## Document Type
ICRU international consensus report — replaces ICRU Report 38 (1985) for cervix brachytherapy.

## Scope
- Three-dimensional image-guided brachytherapy (3D-IGBT)
- Pulse-dose-rate (PDR), high-dose-rate (HDR), and low-dose-rate (LDR) intracavitary and combined intracavitary/interstitial (IC/IS) BT
- GEC-ESTRO target and OAR definitions are adopted

## Target Volume Definitions (Adopted from GEC-ESTRO)
- GTV_res (residual gross tumor at BT)
- HR-CTV (High-Risk Clinical Target Volume)
- IR-CTV (Intermediate-Risk Clinical Target Volume)
- TraC (Treated Volume) — enclosed by prescription isodose
- Points A, P, IR, etc. retained for historical reporting

## Recommended Dose Reporting
- Total dose expressed as **EQD2** (α/β = 10 Gy for tumor, α/β = 3 Gy for OAR)
- Standard OAR parameters: D0.1cc, D1cc, D2cc
- Recommended minimum parameters: HR-CTV D90, D98; IR-CTV D90; OAR D2cc
- Total reference air kerma (TRAK), total treatment time

## EQD2 Formula
EQD2 = D × (d + (α/β)) / (2 + (α/β))
where D = total physical dose, d = dose per fraction, α/β = 3 Gy (OAR) or 10 Gy (tumor)

## Clinical Implementation
- MRI is the gold standard; CT is acceptable if MRI is unavailable
- Planning should be performed on the first BT fraction image
- Adaptive planning: re-imaging and re-planning for major tumor regression or applicator shifts

## Significance
- Globally adopted standard since 2016
- Underpins GEC-ESTRO, ABS, NCCN, and ESGO/ESTRO/ESP cervix cancer guidelines
- Foundation for EMBRACE-I and EMBRACE-II
