# ICRU Report 89: Prescribing, Recording, and Reporting Brachytherapy for Cancer of the Cervix

**Publisher:** International Commission on Radiation Units and Measurements (ICRU)

**Year:** 2013

**URL:** https://www.icru.org/home/reports/prescribing-recording-and-reporting-brachytherapy-for-cancer-of-the-cervix-report-89

---

## Summary

ICRU Report 89 provides standardized recommendations for prescribing, recording, and reporting brachytherapy for cervical cancer. It builds on ICRU Report 38 (1985) and incorporates 3D image-based (MRI) treatment planning concepts from GEC-ESTRO.

## Key Parameters Defined

- **D90**: Minimum dose to 90% of the HR-CTV (primary dose reporting parameter)
- **D100**: Minimum dose to 100% of the HR-CTV
- **D2cc**: Minimum dose to the most exposed 2 cm3 of each organ at risk (bladder, rectum, sigmoid)
- **D1cc**: Minimum dose to the most exposed 1 cm3
- **D0.1cc**: Minimum dose to the most exposed 0.1 cm3
- **HR-CTV**: High-Risk Clinical Target Volume (includes GTV + cervix at time of BT)
- **IR-CTV**: Intermediate-Risk Clinical Target Volume (includes HR-CTV + surrounding tissue at risk)

## Recommendations

- Report D90 and D100 for HR-CTV and IR-CTV
- Report D2cc for bladder, rectum, sigmoid, and small bowel
- Use MRI-based contouring when possible
- Report total dose (EBRT + BT) using equivalent dose in 2 Gy fractions (EQD2)
- Report treatment time (overall treatment time)

## Notes for downstream agent

ICRU 89 is the standard reference for cervix BT dose reporting. Essential for understanding D90/D2cc parameters used in EMBRACE and ABS guidelines.
