## Key Recommendations

### Treatment planning volumes (GEC-ESTRO consensus)
- **GTV (Gross Tumor Volume):** Visible/palpable/pictorial tumor extent at time of brachytherapy (MR T2-weighted). Used for dose-painting escalation.
- **HR-CTV (High-Risk Clinical Target Volume):** Includes GTV + entire cervix + parametrial invasion + uterine body extension suspicious for residual disease. Assessed at each BT fraction.
- **IR-CTV (Intermediate-Risk CTV):** HR-CTV + initial (pre-treatment) tumor extent + a margin of 0.5–1.5 cm to account for microscopic disease.

### Dose prescription (combined EBRT + BT in EQD2)
- **EBRT:** 45–50 Gy in 1.8 Gy fractions, with or without nodal boost (55–60 Gy to involved nodes).
- **Brachytherapy:** Typically 4 fractions × 7 Gy (or 5 × 6 Gy) to HR-CTV D90, integrated with EBRT.
- **HR-CTV D90 cumulative EQD2 (α/β=10):** ≥ 85 Gy (aim 90 Gy for large tumors).
- **IR-CTV D90 cumulative EQD2 (α/β=10):** ≥ 60–65 Gy.
- **Point A dose:** legacy, still reportable but NOT primary planning aim.

### OAR constraints (cumulative EQD2, α/β=3)
- **Bladder D2cc:** ≤ 80–90 Gy (aim ≤ 80 Gy; per ICRU 89).
- **Rectum D2cc:** ≤ 60–65 Gy (aim ≤ 65 Gy; per ICRU 89).
- **Sigmoid D2cc:** ≤ 60–65 Gy (aim ≤ 65 Gy).
- **Bowel D2cc:** ≤ 60–65 Gy (aim ≤ 65 Gy).
- **Vagina PIBS/PIBS+2 cm:** Surface dose constraint; vaginal mucosa D2cc ≤ 140–150 Gy EQD2.

### Applicator recommendations
- IC (intracavitary): tandem + ovoids / tandem + ring / tandem + cylinder.
- IC+IS (hybrid): Vienna / T&O / ring with interstitial needles; allows HR-CTV coverage while sparing OAR.
- IS (interstitial): perineal template (MUPIT, Syed), freehand or laparoscopic.

### Adaptive planning
- One plan per BT fraction is recommended; cumulative dose re-evaluated after each fraction.
- Image modality: MRI preferred (T2-weighted); CT acceptable with CTV delineated using clinical info.

## Notes
- Dose is accumulated using linear-quadratic (LQ) model with α/β=10 for tumor and α/β=3 for OARs.
- 1 fraction per day, 6–16 h apart, preferably with pulses synchronized to EBRT.
- 3D-printed applicators are emerging; not yet standard-of-care per GEC-ESTRO 2018.
