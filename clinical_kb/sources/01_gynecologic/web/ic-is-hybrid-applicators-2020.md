## Indications for IC+IS (Hybrid)
1. Large HR-CTV (>30 cc) not adequately covered by IC alone.
2. Distal parametrial extension (HR-CTV > 5 cm lateral to tandem).
3. Poor response to EBRT (residual disease >4 cm).
4. Asymmetric disease (e.g., unilateral parametrium).
5. HR-CTV adjacent to OAR — interstitial needles allow dose painting and OAR sparing.
6. Narrow vagina precluding ovoid placement (use ring + needles).
7. Remnant disease after EBRT (R-ICB persists despite 50 Gy).

## Vienna Applicator
- Tandem + ring + 9 interstitial needles (placed in ring holes, parallel to tandem).
- Titanium or plastic; MRI-compatible versions available.
- Dose-painting: needles loaded based on HR-CTV extent.

## Utrecht Applicator (T&O + needles)
- Tandem + ovoid with peripheral holes for needles.
- Steeper needle angle (15–25° off tandem axis).

## Tandem & Ring (Capri, Varian)
- Ring with needle channels.
- Allows ± 30° lateral needle angles.

## Freehand Interstitial (no template)
- Used for small-volume disease or post-hysterectomy cases.
- Freehand needle placement guided by transrectal US (TRUS) or laparoscopic visualization.

## Dosimetric Advantages (vs IC alone)
- HR-CTV D90 can be escalated to 90–95 Gy EQD2 with similar OAR D2cc.
- Typical improvement: D90 +5–10 Gy EQD2 with same D2cc, OR same D90 with D2cc −5–10 Gy.
- Critical for tumors >4 cm, FIGO IIB with distal parametrial invasion.

## Clinical Outcomes
- LC at 3 years: 91–95% (large HR-CTV cohort).
- Grade ≥3 morbidity: 8–12% (comparable to IC alone with lower dose).

## Procedural Workflow
1. Pre-plan: T2w MRI to assess HR-CTV geometry, simulate needle positions.
2. Under spinal/general anesthesia, insert applicator and needles.
3. Imaging: MRI or CT for verification.
4. Contour HR-CTV, IR-CTV, OARs (T2w MRI preferred).
5. Inverse planning (IPSA, HIPO, BrachyVision) to optimize dwell times.
6. Treatment: HDR/PDR after loading.

## Dose Schedules
- 4 × 7 Gy HR-CTV D90
- 3 × 8.5 Gy
- 5 × 6 Gy
- PDR: 0.5 Gy/hour continuous over 24–48 h × 1–2 pulses per day

## 3D-Printed Applicators (Emerging)
- Patient-specific applicators (PSA) with custom needle paths.
- Preliminary data: superior conformality, especially for post-hysterectomy cases.
- 3D printing QA: leakage, structure integrity, sterilization validation required.
