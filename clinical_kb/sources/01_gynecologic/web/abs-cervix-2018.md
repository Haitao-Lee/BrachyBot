## Indications
- Locally advanced cervical cancer (FIGO IB2–IVA) treated with definitive chemoradiation: BT is an ESSENTIAL component of curative therapy.
- Postoperative BT considered in selected high-risk (positive margins, parametrial involvement, +LVSI) — but EBRT is primary.

## Recommended Dose Schedules (HDR)

| Schedule | HR-CTV D90 (cumulative EQD2) |
|---|---|
| 5 × 5.5–6.0 Gy (after 45 Gy EBRT) | 80–90 Gy |
| 4 × 7.0 Gy | 85–90 Gy |
| 3 × 8.5 Gy | 80–90 Gy |
| 6 × 5.0 Gy | 80–90 Gy |

Aim for HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2 (α/β=10); 90 Gy for tumors >4 cm.

## Applicator Selection
- **Tandem & ovoids (T&O):** standard for most cases; high dose-rate.
- **Tandem & ring (T&R):** symmetric dose distribution, good for barrel-shaped cervices.
- **Vienna / hybrid (T&O + needles):** advanced cases, large HR-CTV, poor response to EBRT.
- **Interstitial (MUPIT/Syed/template):** extensive parametrial disease, recurrent, post-hysterectomy.
- **Vaginal cylinder:** postoperative, vaginal cuff, or adjuvant.

## Imaging
- MRI strongly preferred for target definition (T2-weighted).
- CT acceptable if MRI unavailable (with reduced HR-CTV accuracy).
- US can be used for real-time applicator guidance during insertion.

## 2022 Update Highlights
- Reinforced 90 Gy HR-CTV D90 as primary aim.
- Added detailed planning aims for IC+IS hybrid applicators.
- Added guidance on dose accumulation and plan-of-the-day.

## Special Considerations
- PDR (pulsed-dose-rate) regimens mirror HDR cumulative doses: ~0.5–0.6 Gy/hour continuous over 24–48 h.
- Daily fractions, ≥6 hours apart, 2–4 fractions/week.
- Pediatric and young patients: ovarian transposition may be considered.
