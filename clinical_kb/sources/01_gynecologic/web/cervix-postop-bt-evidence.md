## GOG 92 (Keys 2003, Post-Op Pelvic RT)
- **Population:** 277 patients with early-stage cervical cancer after radical hysterectomy with intermediate-risk features.
- **Intervention:** Pelvic EBRT (50.4 Gy) vs no further therapy.
- **Result:** 2-year recurrence-free survival 88% (RT) vs 79% (observation). p=0.008.
- **Established:** adjuvant EBRT after radical hysterectomy for intermediate-risk patients.

## GOG 99 (Sedlis 1999) — Sedlis Criteria
Defined intermediate-risk ("Sedlis criteria") for which adjuvant RT recommended:
- **Positive pelvic nodes, parametrial involvement, OR positive margins:** HIGH-risk (chemoRT).
- **Combination of:** LVSI, deep stromal invasion (≥1/3), large tumor size (>4 cm).
  - LVSI + DSI + >4 cm: 1 risk factor
  - LVSI + DSI: 2 risk factors
  - LVSI + >4 cm: 2 risk factors
  - DSI + >4 cm: 1 risk factor
  - ≥2 risk factors → adjuvant EBRT

## Adjuvant BT (Vaginal Cuff Boost) After EBRT
- **Indication:** Positive margins OR vaginal involvement.
- **Dose:** 3 × 6–7 Gy to 5 mm depth from cylinder surface (HDR).
- **Alternative:** 5 × 4.5 Gy to 5 mm depth.
- **EQD2 (α/β=3):** ~ 50–60 Gy to vaginal mucosa (cumulative with EBRT).
- **Cumulative dose aim:** vaginal mucosa 80–100 Gy EQD2.

## High-Risk Subgroup (Positive Nodes, Parametrial, Margins)
- **GOG 109 / Intergroup 0107:** Cisplatin + 5-FU + RT vs RT alone. 4-year OS 71% vs 64% (HR 0.74).
- **Established:** Concurrent chemoradiation is standard of care.
- BT boost: consider if positive vaginal margin (per NCCN).

## Vaginal Cuff Boost Techniques
1. **Cylinder (single channel):** 28–35 mm diameter, dosed to 5 mm depth.
2. **Multichannel cylinder:** for asymmetric or longer treatment.
3. **Ovoids:** rarely used post-op.

## Sequencing of EBRT + BT
- EBRT first (45–50 Gy), then BT boost.
- If positive margins: VCB may be given concurrently (or as soon as EBRT finishes).
- Total time EBRT start → BT end < 8 weeks preferred.

## Postoperative Brachytherapy Outcome
- Vaginal recurrence rate: 5–10% with EBRT alone → <2% with EBRT + VCB (in positive margin).
- 5-year OS: 70–80% in intermediate-risk, 60–70% in high-risk (with chemoRT).
- Chronic toxicity: vaginal stenosis 20–30%, dyspareunia, fistula <5%.
