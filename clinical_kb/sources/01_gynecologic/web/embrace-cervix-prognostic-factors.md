## Stage and LC
| FIGO stage | 5-yr LC (EMBRACE-I) | 5-yr OS |
|---|---|---|
| IB1/IB2 | 96% | 88% |
| IIA | 92% | 80% |
| IIB | 91% | 76% |
| IIIA | 86% | 65% |
| IIIB | 87% | 65% |
| IIIC1 (pelvic nodes) | 90% | 73% |
| IIIC2 (PA nodes) | 80% | 55% |
| IVA | 75% | 45% |

## Tumor Size
- <2 cm: 5-yr LC 98%
- 2–4 cm: 95%
- 4–6 cm: 89%
- >6 cm: 80%

## HR-CTV D90 (Cumulative EQD2)
- <85 Gy: 5-yr LC 84%
- 85–90 Gy: 92%
- >90 Gy: 95%

**Conclusion:** D90 ≥ 90 Gy is the planning aim for tumors >4 cm.

## Treatment Time
- Total treatment time (start EBRT → end BT) > 56 days → 1% LC loss per day.
- Aim: complete in < 50 days.

## Nodal Status
- IIIC1: 5-yr LC 90% (boost to positive nodes).
- IIIC2: 5-yr LC 80% (worse outcome).
- Para-aortic RT recommended for IIIC2 + IIIC1 with high para-aortic risk.

## HR-CTV at Time of BT
- HR-CTV at 1st BT < 20 cc: 5-yr LC 95%.
- HR-CTV at 1st BT 20–40 cc: 90%.
- HR-CTV at 1st BT > 40 cc: 80%.

## Response to EBRT
- Complete response (no visible tumor at BT): 5-yr LC 96%.
- Partial response: 89%.
- Stable disease: 70%.

## Histology
- Squamous: 5-yr OS 75%.
- Adenocarcinoma: 65%.
- Adenosquamous: 70%.

## EMBRACE-II Outcomes (2024 update)
- 5-yr LC 95% (all stages, vs 92% in EMBRACE-I).
- 5-yr OS 80% (vs 74% in EMBRACE-I).
- HR-CTV D90 median 92 Gy EQD2 (vs 89 Gy in EMBRACE-I).
- Rectum D2cc median 60 Gy EQD2 (vs 62 Gy in EMBRACE-I).
- Bladder D2cc median 76 Gy EQD2 (vs 80 Gy in EMBRACE-I).
- Confirms tighter planning aims improve therapeutic ratio.

## Key Predictive Factors
1. HR-CTV D90 (most important modifiable factor).
2. Tumor size at diagnosis (>4 cm = worse).
3. Nodal status (IIIC2 = worse).
4. Response to EBRT.
5. Total treatment time.
