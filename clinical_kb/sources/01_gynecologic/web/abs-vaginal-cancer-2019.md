## Epidemiology and Background
- Primary vaginal cancer is rare (~1–2% of gynecologic malignancies).
- Squamous cell carcinoma is most common (85–90%); then adenocarcinoma (clear cell, endometrioid); then melanoma, sarcoma.
- Stage FIGO I (limited to vaginal wall) has best prognosis.

## Treatment Overview
- **Stage I (small, <2 cm, superficial):** BT ALONE can be curative (5-year LC 85–95%).
- **Stage I (deeper or >2 cm) and II–IV:** EBRT + BT boost.

## Applicator Selection
| Lesion location | Preferred applicator |
|---|---|
| Upper vagina | Multichannel cylinder OR cylinder + interstitial needles |
| Mid/distal vagina | Multichannel cylinder OR cylinder + interstitial needles |
| Circumferential | Multichannel cylinder (preferred over single-channel) |
| Infiltrative / bulky | Interstitial (perineal template or freehand) |
| Vault recurrence | Cylinder + interstitial or perineal template |

## Dose (definitive, EBRT + BT)
- **EBRT:** 45–50 Gy / 25 fx whole pelvis + vaginal cuff.
- **BT boost:**
  - Cylinder: 3 × 5–7 Gy to surface (or 5 mm depth); cumulative EQD2 ≈ 70–80 Gy to HR-CTV.
  - Interstitial: 4 × 5–6 Gy to HR-CTV D90; cumulative EQD2 ≈ 75–85 Gy.
  - Multichannel cylinder: dose-painting allows 5–10 mm depth optimization.

## OAR Constraints (cumulative EQD2, α/β=3)
- **Vagina (D2cc):** ≤ 120–140 Gy EQD2
- **Bladder (D2cc):** ≤ 80 Gy EQD2
- **Rectum (D2cc):** ≤ 65 Gy EQD2
- **Urethra (D0.1cc or D10):** ≤ 120–140 Gy EQD2
- **Sigmoid / Bowel (D2cc):** ≤ 65 Gy EQD2

## Postoperative Vaginal Cuff BT (VCE for Endometrial)
- **Indication:** Selected stage I endometrioid (high-intermediate risk per PORTEC-3 criteria: age ≥ 60 + grade 3; or age ≥ 50 + grade 3 + ≥ 1 risk factor; or stage II).
- **Applicator:** cylinder (28–35 mm diameter).
- **Dose:** 6 × 5 Gy to 5 mm depth (3D image-based) OR 3 × 7 Gy to 5 mm depth OR 5 × 4.5 Gy.
- **Alternative:** 3 × 10 Gy in 2 fractions/week (vaginal mucosa-sparing protocol).
- **Aim:** VCB (vaginal cuff brachytherapy) reduces vaginal recurrence from ~10% to <2%.

## Special Considerations
- **HDR vs PDR:** HDR is standard in US; PDR equivalent dose schedules in Europe.
- **Multichannel cylinder:** preferred for asymmetric disease or to spare anterior rectal wall.
- **Ovarian transposition:** consider for premenopausal women.
