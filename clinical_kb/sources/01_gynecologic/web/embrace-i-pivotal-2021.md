## Study Design
- **Population:** 1416 patients with FIGO stage IB–IVB cervical cancer
- **Centers:** 24 centers across Europe, North America, and Asia
- **Enrollment:** 2008–2015
- **Treatment:** EBRT (45–50 Gy ± nodal boost) + concurrent cisplatin + MRI-based IGABT (4 × 7 Gy HR-CTV)

## Outcomes (5-year actuarial)

### Local control and survival
| Endpoint | 5-year rate |
|---|---|
| Local control (LC) | 92% (stage IIB: 91%, IIIB: 87%) |
| Overall survival (OS) | 74% (stage IIB: 76%, IIIB: 65%) |
| Disease-free survival | 68% |
| Pelvic control | 87% |

### Morbidity (5-year cumulative, grade 3–5, per CTCAE)
- Rectal: GI grade ≥3 = 8.5%
- Bladder: GU grade ≥3 = 6.8%
- Vaginal stenosis: 9–27% (underreported; patient-reported higher)

### D90 / D2cc distributions (median)
- HR-CTV D90 (EQD2 α/β=10): 89.4 Gy (IQR 84.5–94.4)
- IR-CTV D90 (EQD2 α/β=10): 68.2 Gy
- Bladder D2cc (EQD2 α/β=3): 79.7 Gy
- Rectum D2cc (EQD2 α/β=3): 62.3 Gy
- Sigmoid D2cc (EQD2 α/β=3): 60.5 Gy

## Key Conclusions
- MRI-based IGABT achieves excellent LC (92% overall) with acceptable late morbidity.
- HR-CTV D90 is a strong prognostic factor: <85 Gy associated with worse LC.
- OAR D2cc strongly correlated with late toxicity; planning aims should respect GEC-ESTRO 2018 limits.
- EMBRACE-II is the successor study applying even more constrained planning aims to improve therapeutic ratio.

## Dose-response findings
- LC plateaus around HR-CTV D90 of 85–90 Gy EQD2.
- Each 5 Gy increase in HR-CTV D90 = ~3% LC gain up to 90 Gy.
- D2cc rectum > 75 Gy EQD2 = sharp rise in grade ≥2 rectal bleeding.
- D2cc bladder > 100 Gy EQD2 = grade ≥2 cystitis markedly increases.
