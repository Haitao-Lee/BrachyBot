## Brachytherapy Indications (Definitive)
- **Standard of care for FIGO IB2, IIA2, IIB, IIIB, IIIC, IVA** after external beam radiation therapy (EBRT) with concurrent cisplatin.
- BT boost can be omitted ONLY in rare, exophytic <1 cm tumors, but EBRT + BT is preferred.
- Total combined (EBRT + BT) point A dose equivalent: 80–85 Gy for small tumors, 85–90+ Gy for large (>4 cm).

## Treatment Schema
1. **EBRT:** 45 Gy / 25 fx to pelvis (± para-aortic nodes if indicated).
2. **Concurrent chemotherapy:** Cisplatin 40 mg/m² weekly × 5 (or other platinum regimens).
3. **Brachytherapy:** HDR or PDR boost, starting ≤ 7 weeks after EBRT start.
4. **Image guidance:** MRI strongly preferred (T2w); CT acceptable.

## Postoperative Adjuvant Setting
- **Sedlis criteria** (intermediate risk): EBRT alone (45 Gy) is usually sufficient — no routine BT.
- **Positive margins / parametrial involvement / nodes:** EBRT ± BT boost to vaginal cuff (3 × 6–7 Gy at 5 mm depth, or 5 × 4.5–5 Gy).

## Dose/Schedule Examples (HDR)
- 5 × 5.5–6.0 Gy to HR-CTV D90 (after 45 Gy EBRT).
- 4 × 7.0 Gy to HR-CTV D90.
- 3 × 8.5 Gy to HR-CTV D90 (institutional).

## OAR Constraints
Per GEC-ESTRO 2018 / ABS 2018:
- Bladder D2cc ≤ 80–90 Gy EQD2
- Rectum D2cc ≤ 65–75 Gy EQD2
- Sigmoid D2cc ≤ 65–75 Gy EQD2
- Bowel D2cc ≤ 65 Gy EQD2

## Special Populations
- **Stage IVB (distant mets):** BT can provide palliation of bleeding/discharge.
- **Pregnancy:** BT deferred until fetal delivery; EBRT can be modified.
- **Elderly / comorbid:** can use weekly cisplatin OR carboplatin; BT schedule may be reduced.

## Practice Recommendations
- Each center should have a structured QA program for applicator commissioning, planning, and OAR contouring.
- Image-based planning (MRI or CT) is the standard, NOT 2D point-A-based planning.
- Track HR-CTV D90, IR-CTV D90, and OAR D2cc as institutional quality metrics.
