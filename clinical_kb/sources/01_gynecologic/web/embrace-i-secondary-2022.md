## Rectum Dose-Response (Schmid 2020)
- 5-year grade ≥2 rectal bleeding rate by rectum D2cc:
  - <60 Gy EQD2: 5%
  - 60–70 Gy: 9%
  - 70–80 Gy: 12%
  - >80 Gy: 18%
- **Planning aim:** D2cc rectum ≤ 65 Gy EQD2 (α/β=3) → 5% risk of grade ≥2 bleeding.

## Bladder Dose-Response (Fokdal 2020)
- 5-year grade ≥2 cystitis rate by bladder D2cc:
  - <80 Gy EQD2: 6%
  - 80–90 Gy: 9%
  - 90–100 Gy: 13%
  - >100 Gy: 20%
- **Planning aim:** D2cc bladder ≤ 80 Gy EQD2 (α/β=3).

## Vaginal Stenosis (Kirchheiner 2021)
- 5-year vaginal stenosis (any grade): 27% overall.
- Vaginal D2cc > 140 Gy EQD2 strongly correlated with stenosis.
- Vaginal dilator use post-BT mitigates stenosis risk.
- Aim: limit PIBS+2 cm to ≤ 120 Gy EQD2.

## Uterine Corpus Dose (Westerveld 2020)
- Uterine body extension (UTE) at diagnosis: present in 47% of patients.
- Patients with UTE had similar LC if HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy.
- Uterine corpus D2cc (high-dose region) usually > 90 Gy EQD2 — well-tolerated.

## Key Conclusions
- All OAR D2cc constraints are clinically meaningful and actionable.
- Vaginal toxicity often under-reported; counseling and dilator use are critical.
- Plan-of-the-day adaptation is the strongest tool for meeting OAR constraints.

## Treatment Time
- Total treatment time (EBRT start to BT end) > 56 days → 1% loss in LC per day.
- Aim: complete EBRT + BT in ≤ 50–55 days.
