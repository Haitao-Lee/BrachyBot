## Definition
- **PDR = continuous low-dose-rate-like radiation delivered as hourly pulses (typically 0.4–0.6 Gy/hour average, 1 pulse/hour).**
- Each pulse = ~10–30 min of source dwelling.
- 24 h treatment = 24 pulses.
- Source activity: typically 1 Ci Ir-192 (vs 10 Ci for HDR).

## Clinical Rationale
- Biologically similar to LDR (low-dose-rate 0.4–0.8 Gy/h) due to sub-lethal damage repair during pulse intervals.
- BUT easier to optimize dose distribution than LDR (computer-controlled dwell positions).
- Particularly useful for large-volume interstitial implants in cervix, vagina, vulva.

## Dose Equivalence (LDR ↔ PDR ↔ HDR)
- **LDR classic:** 0.5 Gy/h × 96 h = 48 Gy (cervix).
- **PDR equivalent:** 24–30 Gy in hourly pulses over 2 days.
- **HDR equivalent:** 4 × 7 Gy = 28 Gy (assumes 6h inter-fraction interval).
- EQD2 (α/β=10) for 25 Gy HDR ≈ 27 Gy PDR ≈ 30 Gy LDR for cervix.

## GEC-ESTRO PDR Recommendations
- **Pulse size:** 0.4–0.6 Gy/hour average; max 1.0 Gy per pulse to avoid exceeding LDR radiobiology.
- **Pulse interval:** 1 hour between pulses (60 min).
- **Treatment duration:** 24–48 h continuous.
- **Total dose:** 25–35 Gy PDR in 1–2 days (after 45 Gy EBRT) for cervix.

## Clinical Use
- Mostly European centers (Netherlands, Belgium, Germany, France).
- Practical advantages: dose painting, fewer fractions, outpatient over 2 days.
- Disadvantages: requires inpatient bed, more expensive equipment, complex QA.

## OAR Constraints (PDR)
Same as HDR:
- Rectum D2cc: ≤ 65 Gy EQD2 (α/β=3)
- Bladder D2cc: ≤ 80 Gy EQD2 (α/β=3)
- Sigmoid D2cc: ≤ 65 Gy EQD2 (α/β=3)

## Common PDR Schedules (cervix)
- **Single implant, 2 days:** 24 Gy (48 pulses of 0.5 Gy) + EBRT 45 Gy = 70–75 Gy EQD2.
- **Two implants, 1 day each:** 15 Gy × 2 (separated by 1 week) + EBRT 45 Gy.
- **Boost after EBRT:** 25–30 Gy PDR boost.

## PDR for Endometrium
- 25–30 Gy in 1 session (24 h).
- OR 5 × 5 Gy in 5 separate sessions (less common).

## 2024 Update
- PDR is being phased out in many centers in favor of HDR with proper fractionation.
- Some centers retain PDR for interstitial implants requiring extended treatment time.
- Source: Ir-192 (low activity 1 Ci) used in older PDR units.
- Newer "electronic brachytherapy" (e.g., Esteya) may replace PDR for superficial sites.
