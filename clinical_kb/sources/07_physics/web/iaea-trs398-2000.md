## IAEA TRS-398 (BT Addendum)

### Reference Dosimetry for BT

- **Reference quantity for BT**: Reference air kerma rate (RAKR) at 1 m, in µGy·h⁻¹
- **Dose-rate constant Λ**: dose rate in water at r=1 cm per unit RAKR (in cGy·h⁻¹ / (µGy·h⁻¹) = cGy/µGy)
  - Modern convention: Λ = Ḋ(1 cm, 90°) / S_k
- **Traceability**: dosimetry should be traceable to BIPM (through PSDL like NIST, NPL, PTB, NRC, ARPANSA)

### Calibration of Well Chambers

- Well-type ionization chamber calibration factor N_(RAKR,M) traceable to primary standards
- For ¹⁹²Ir HDR, the calibration is typically provided in terms of:
  - **N_(sk,D_w)**: air-kerma strength calibration factor (Gy·m²·C⁻¹)
  - Or N_R (reference air kerma) calibration
- Correction factors for ion recombination, polarity, pressure/temperature, source decay

## IAEA TECDOC-1275 — BT Source Calibration

### Scope

- HDR ¹⁹²Ir (Ir-192 HDR)
- LDR ¹³⁷Cs, ⁶⁰Co
- Eye plaques (¹⁰⁶Ru, ¹²⁵I, ¹⁰³Pd)
- Beta and low-energy photon sources

### Calibration Methods

- **In-air measurement** with standard free-air chamber (impractical for ¹⁹²Ir, used for low-energy)
- **Well-type ionization chamber** (most common for HDR/LDR)
- **Solid phantom** (e.g., PMMA) with calibrated ionization chamber for cross-check
- **Farmer-type chamber in water** at reference distance for LDR (¹³⁷Cs, ⁶⁰Co)

### Recommended Tolerance

- Source strength agreement between user and manufacturer: ± 5%
- Independent verification strongly recommended before clinical use

## Clinical Implementation

- TRS-398/TECDOC-1275 are the international basis for BT source calibration
- Consistent with AAPM TG-40, TG-148 (LDR), and TG-51 HDR addendum
- AAPM 2020 brachytherapy physics primer recommends TRS-398 framework for international practice

## Key Equations

- Reference air kerma rate: K̇_R (µGy·h⁻¹) at 1 m
- S_k (U) = K̇_R (µGy·h⁻¹) (numerically equal; 1 U = 1 µGy·m²·h⁻¹ = 1 cGy·cm²·h⁻¹)
- D(r,θ) = Λ · K̇_R · (r₀/r)² · g(r) · F(r,θ), where Λ has absorbed-dose-to-water basis
