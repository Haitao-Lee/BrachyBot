## Key Concepts

### Volume Definitions

- **GTV (Gross Tumor Volume)**: visible/palpable tumor at brachytherapy (typically the residual tumor at time of BT, after EBRT)
- **HR-CTV (High-Risk Clinical Target Volume)**: GTV + entire cervix + parametrial disease + adjacent uterine body, at time of BT; corresponds to region of presumed highest tumor burden
- **IR-CTV (Intermediate-Risk CTV)**: HR-CTV + a 5–10 mm margin, capturing initial disease extent and adjacent tissues at risk of spread
- **TraC (Treated Volume)**: volume enclosed by the prescribed isodose
- **OAR volumes**: bladder, rectum, sigmoid, bowel

### Dose Reporting

For each OAR, report D2cc, D1cc, D0.1cc (minimum dose to the most exposed 2 cm³, 1 cm³, 0.1 cm³) in **EQD2 (Equivalent Dose in 2 Gy fractions)** using α/β of 3 Gy for OARs and 10 Gy for tumor.

### Recommended Prescription for Cervical Cancer (ICRU 89, GEC-ESTRO 2018)

- HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2(α/β=10) for locally advanced disease, 90 Gy for very large/poor-responding tumors
- IR-CTV D90 ≥ 60 Gy EQD2(α/β=10)
- OAR constraints (combined EBRT + BT):
  - D2cc rectum < 65–75 Gy EQD2(α/β=3)
  - D2cc bladder < 80–90 Gy EQD2(α/β=3)
  - D2cc sigmoid < 65–75 Gy EQD2(α/β=3)
  - D2cc bowel < 65–75 Gy EQD2(α/β=3)
- Typical HR-CTV dose rate 0.6–0.8 Gy/h
- 4 fractions × 6–7 Gy over 2–3 weeks, twice daily with ≥ 6 h gap

### Point Dose Reporting (Legacy)

- Point A (right, left): 2 cm superior to external os, 2 cm lateral to tandem
- Point B (right, left): 2 cm superior to external os, 5 cm lateral to tandem (obturator vessels)
- Bladder point: posterior surface of balloon on lateral radiograph
- Rectum point: 5 mm posterior to posterior vaginal wall on lateral
- ICRU 38/89 recommends contouring over point doses where possible

### Time Pattern of BT

- Pulsed: 4×7 Gy HDR or 5×6 Gy HDR
- PDR: hourly pulses of 0.5–0.7 Gy
- LDR: 20–30 Gy in continuous 50–70 cGy/h
- Combined with EBRT 45–50 Gy
