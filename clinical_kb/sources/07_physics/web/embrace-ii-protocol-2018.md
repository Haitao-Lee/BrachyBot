## EMBRACE-II — Protocol Highlights

### Patient Population

- Locally advanced cervical cancer (FIGO IIB-IVA), high-risk early stage
- Squamous cell, adenocarcinoma, adenosquamous

### EBRT Prescription

- **45–50 Gy / 25 fx** to pelvis
- **Simultaneous Integrated Boost (SIB)**: 55–60 Gy EQD2 to involved nodes
- **Parametrial boost**: optional, 5.4–10.8 Gy

### BT Prescription

- **HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy EQD2(α/β=10)**, target 90 Gy
- **4 fractions × 7 Gy** HR-CTV D90 (or 5 × 6 Gy)
- **Dose rate 0.6–0.8 Gy/h** effective (if LDR-equivalent)
- **PDR 50–60 pulses × 0.6 Gy**
- **Timing**: BT starts after EBRT (typically last 2–3 weeks)

### Adaptive Re-planning

- Re-plan every fraction (4 plans total) based on:
  - Applicator position (CBCT or MR)
  - HR-CTV regression
  - OAR position
- OAR constraints:
  - D2cc rectum < 65 Gy EQD2(α/β=3) (aim < 60 Gy)
  - D2cc bladder < 80 Gy EQD2(α/β=3) (aim < 75 Gy)
  - D2cc sigmoid < 65 Gy EQD2(α/β=3)
  - D2cc bowel < 65 Gy EQD2(α/β=3)

### Imaging

- **MRI for BT planning** (T2-weighted) — gold standard
- CT acceptable if MRI contraindicated
- **Per-fraction MRI** for adaptive re-planning (recommended but not always feasible)

### Outcomes (EMBRACE-I)

- 5-year local control: 92% for HR-CTV D90 ≥ 85 Gy
- 5-year OS: 74% for stage IIB, 52% for stage IV
- Late grade 3+ toxicity: rectum 8%, bladder 6%, vagina 14%

## Physics-Specific Protocol Items

### Source

- ¹⁹²Ir HDR (Flexisource, mHDR, VS2000) per institution
- All source types AAPM TG-43 compliant
- Source strength re-verified at each fraction

### Dwell Time / Position

- HR-CTV coverage:
  - D90 ≥ 90 Gy EQD2(α/β=10) target
  - V100 ≥ 90% of HR-CTV
- IR-CTV coverage:
  - D90 ≥ 60 Gy EQD2
- Loading pattern:
  - Tandem + ring or tandem + ovoids (IC)
  - Interstitial needles as needed (IC+IS)
  - Per institutional preference, but GEC-ESTRO guidelines followed

### Dose Calculation

- TG-43 formalism acceptable (homogeneous water assumption)
- MBDCA (Acuros BV) recommended for cases with significant heterogeneity
- Tissue segmentation: 6–9 tissue types (air, soft tissue, fat, muscle, bone, cartilage, applicator materials)

### Reporting

- Cumulative EBRT + BT DVH
- HR-CTV D90, D98, V100, V150, V200
- OAR D2cc, D1cc, D0.1cc
- Total treatment time
- Dose per fraction
- Number of fractions
- Applicator type
- Imaging modality used

## Key Contributions to Clinical Physics

1. **Established HR-CTV D90 as the standard dosimetric parameter** for cervical BT
2. **EQD2 summation** of EBRT and BT dose with α/β=10 for tumor, α/β=3 for OAR
3. **Adaptive re-planning** is now considered standard of care
4. **MRI-guided BT** is preferred for GTV/HR-CTV delineation
5. **Parametrial dose** correlates with local control
6. **TRAK** is a useful parameter for inter-fraction consistency
