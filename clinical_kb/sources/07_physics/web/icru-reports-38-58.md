## ICRU Report 38 (1985) — Key Concepts

### Reference Points (Manchester-style)

- **Point A**: defined by Tod & Meredith (1938) — 2 cm superior to the external os, 2 cm lateral to the central canal of the uterus
  - Classic reference for cervical cancer IC BT
  - Approximates location of uterine artery crossing of ureter
- **Point B**: 2 cm superior to external os, 5 cm lateral
- **Bladder reference point**: Foley balloon, posterior surface, AP-lateral intersection
- **Rectal reference point**: 5 mm behind posterior vaginal wall, at level of lower uterine segment
- **Pelvic wall (left/right)**, **lymphatic trapezoid** (Fletcher): historical

### Total Reference Air Kerma (TRAK)

- Sum of air-kerma strength × dwell time products across all dwell positions (in µGy·m²)
- Correlates with integral dose; useful for reporting total BT load

### Prescription

- Typical: 75–80 Gy to Point A (combined EBRT + BT, low dose rate equivalent)
- LDR: 0.4–0.8 Gy/h at Point A
- HDR: ~6–8 Gy × 4–5 fractions, twice daily

## ICRU Report 58 (1997) — Key Concepts for Interstitial BT

### Volume Definitions

- **GTV (Gross Tumor Volume)**: macroscopic/palpable/visible
- **CTV (Clinical Target Volume)**: GTV + margin of subclinical disease
- **PTV (Planning Target Volume)**: CTV + margin for setup/organ motion (often minimal in BT due to applicator stabilization)
- **Treated Volume (TV)**: volume enclosed by prescription isodose
- **Irradiated Volume (IV)**: volume receiving ≥ 50% of prescription dose

### Dose Uniformity

- For interstitial: target dose uniformity ratio (D_max / D_min) ≤ 1.5–1.7 preferred
- **Minimum target dose (MTD)**: lowest dose in CTV; report as D_min
- **Mean central dose (MCD)**: average dose at the centroid of sources
- **High-dose volume (HDV)**: volume receiving > 150% of MCD; should be minimized
- **Coverage index (CI)**: % of CTV receiving prescription dose (should be > 90%)
- **External volume index (EVI)**: ratio of TV to CTV (lower is better; 1.0 ideal)

### Dose Specification

- Report dose to:
  - Mean central dose (MCD)
  - Minimum target dose (D_min)
  - Dose uniformity index
  - D90, V100, V150, V200 (modern metrics)
  - OAR doses (max, D2cc, D1cc, D0.1cc)

## Modern Usage

- ICRU 38/58/89 are foundational; modern practice (GEC-ESTRO 2005, 2018, 2022) has shifted to volume-based 3D planning (HR-CTV, IR-CTV, D90, D2cc) rather than point doses
- Point A remains useful for inter-clinic comparison and historical context
